# Grade 3 — Language Arts — Term 1 — Unit 1

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Language Arts, Term 1, Unit 1 (6 weeks). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Subject:** Language Arts
**Term:** 1
**Unit:** 1 (6 weeks)

## Introduction to the Unit

The Grade Three students, having completed Grades One and Two, are being exposed to various strategies and skills of Language Arts that will further their understanding of how language works. In this unit, the students will be given opportunities to practise their listening and speaking skills through a variety of engaging activities. In reading, they apply previously learnt skills to improve word recognition and vocabulary. As students improve fluency, they apply the strategies learnt to increase their comprehension. They will also demonstrate their understanding through the use of graphic organizers and outlines. They use self-questioning strategies to engage in active reading.

Students incorporate the use of negative forms of the verbs "to be" and "to do", and the use of the conjunction "but" in a range of contexts to show the opposite or conflicting ideas. Their writing incorporates the use of descriptive adjectives to show comparison when comparing two or more things.

Students continue to use the structures of the English Language to write simple Language Experience Stories. They develop their pre-writing skills with the aid of graphic organizers. Their drafts are more detailed and application of skills learnt across strands is evident.

This unit's reading and talking content is linked to the Integrated Studies theme **"My Body"** (caring for the body, healthy foods).

## Strand: Speaking and Listening

### Attainment Targets
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively.
- Listen to, recall, understand and respond to speakers' messages, whether implicit or explicit.
- Recognise, value and make distinctions between home language and SJE to improve/acquire language and literacy competencies.
- Explain and comment on speakers' use of language, including use of SJE and JC, and their use of vocabulary, grammar and other features.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Observe rules for listening and speaking in different settings/contexts.
- Listen and retell short stories using SJE.
- Listen to stories from various media.
- Listen and follow instructions accurately.
- Report on an incident observed or discussed.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Revisit rules for speaking and listening. Reinforce rules for listening to and speaking with others. Discuss the purpose of listening. Talk about setting the environment for listening and speaking.
- Use listening games to provide cognitive connections — e.g. *Simon Says*: "wait for me", "stand beside the door", "put the ball under the table".
- Participate in guided discussion using standard language in talk about self.
- Do controlled talk to scaffold students as they give information about self (teeth, stomach and lungs). Discuss how and why it is important to care for the different parts of our bodies (use the Language Experience Approach to scaffold or direct talk). Recall the importance of eating healthy foods and daily exercise.
- Listen to a story to tell the order of events. Use SJE to retell the order of events in the story.
- Listen to stories from various media and identify the conflict (problem) in the story and say how the problem was solved. Suggest other alternatives to solving the problem. In groups, students role play their suggestions.
- Listen to instructions and fold paper to complete making a paper boat/fan/plane/car.
- Select from a list an activity that they have never done. They will say "I have never...". Other students who have done this activity will share orally with the class.
- Discuss the challenges they had while they were involved in the paper folding activity. Give an oral report on discussions and observations.

### Key Skills
Listen attentively · Respond to instructions · Speak clearly and concisely using SJE · Retell events using SJE · Identify problems · Solve problems · Suggest alternatives · Follow instructions · Report incidents · Discuss challenges

### Assessment
- SJE appropriately used in different contexts of classroom discussions.
- Retold stories adequately expressed in SJE to tell the order of events.
- Problems identified correctly; suggestions given are relevant to the problems; active listening demonstrated in students' ability to complete the given task.
- Instructions followed correctly to complete the product.
- Reports given reflect satisfactorily incidents observed and discussed.

- Instructions for simple origami can be found at origami-instructions.com

## Strand: Word Recognition and Fluency

### Attainment Targets
- Read fluently and with appreciation.
- Automatically recognise words (including basic sight word lists) through repeated exposure and mnemonic devices.
- Establish a concept of print and use a range of word recognition clues, re-reading and reading ahead, to identify new words.
- Develop phonic awareness and use knowledge of letter-sound correspondences in order to decode unfamiliar words.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Identify long vowel sounds and blend these with consonant sounds to read words.
- Read words with consonant blends in the initial and final positions.
- Review and practise the proper pronunciation of 'r-controlled' vowels.
- Revise and use syllabication rules to improve vocabulary and spelling.
- Apply the Word Within Word strategy as a means of deciphering unknown words.
- Identify and explore the meaning of inflectional endings as a means of differentiating between words during the reading process.
- Read in isolation and in context, sight words appropriate to the grade level.
- Review and use spelling/syllabication rules to accurately spell unfamiliar/grade level words.
- Work cooperatively with peers to build vocabulary.
- Classify words into categories.
- Use context clues, pictures, words, sentences, paragraphs as an aid to gain meaning of unfamiliar words.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:

**Phonics**
- Revise the phonics clue "Long vowels say their names" and practise it with a peer. Read a list of words and say which include the long vowel sound of 'a'. Identify where in each word the long 'a' sound occurs. Practise to read words in which the long vowel sound for the letter 'a' occurs in initial, medial and ending positions — e.g. initial position: *apron, April, amen, Avon*; medial position: *sail, whale*.
- Revise blends learnt in Grade 2 by listening to a recording which presents words that begin with consonant blends, then isolate the blends in each word — separating each sound in the blend, then blending the sounds together. Alternatively, listen to the teacher as she models the steps — e.g. *place* → *p-l* → /pl/. (Tip: *Blends are made up of two sounds but each sound in the blend may be separated from the other.*) Follow through with other blends (bl, cr, fr, pr, gr, sl, st, etc.) in subsequent lessons.
- In small groups, examine words on cards that end with blends — for example, *first, think, past, lamp, friend, dusk*. Identify the blend in each word and take turns to pronounce the blend in isolation. Manipulate the blend by separating the individual sounds and then blending them back together. Individually read the set of words, emphasizing the blend at the end of each word. Read sentences which include one or more words with blends in final position.
- Read the tip of the day and then listen to the teacher pronouncing examples of r-controlled words. (Tip: *When a vowel is followed by 'r', the vowel sound changes.*) Examples: *bird, word, corn, smart, sharp, hurt, term, fern*. In pairs, practise to read the words.

**Structural Analysis**
- Use the Syllabication Rule Kit (a box with a set of cards outlining different rules) to revise different syllabication rules. For example:
  - Do not attempt to divide a one-syllable word into syllables — e.g. *girl, wall, sell, just*.
  - Do not separate consonant digraphs and blends into syllables — e.g. *think/ing, church/es*.
  - When two or more consonants occur between two vowels in a word, the syllable is usually divided between the consonants — e.g. *lit/tle, pret/ty, prin/cess*.
  - Divide compound words between the words that make up the compound word.
- In small groups, examine words requiring the application of different syllabication rules, then for each word: indicate the rule which would best suit the syllabication of the word and give the reason; divide the word into syllables (or leave as it stands); count and indicate the number of syllables.
- With a peer, use the steps in the Word-Within-Word Strategy to decode words such as *intelligent, suitable, passage*. Steps: circle the words you recognize in the word and say them aloud (e.g. *in, tell, gent*); pronounce or work out the part of the word that is just a word part/unknown word; put all the different words and the word part together in the correct sequence and use prior knowledge of syllabication rules to pronounce as a whole word.
- In small groups, read word pairs aloud, then discuss what makes each word in the pair different from the other — e.g. *reach/reaches, friend/friends, walk/walking, prepare/prepares*. Share in a whole group activity aimed at emphasizing that inflectional endings make words different both in pronunciation and the meaning communicated. (Tip: *Pronounce all inflectional endings when they occur in words because inflectional endings change words and their meanings.*)

**Sight Words**
- Review sight words using the Word Wall. Play games such as "I SPY" and take turns in challenging peers to identify words indicated by the leader.
- Play the Treasure Hunt game in order to identify sight words. (Words are hidden in different places inside or in a designated area outside — under chairs, in reference books, under stones at the roots of trees, etc. The student who finds the highest number of words, shows each word and accurately reads it aloud, is given a prize.)
- Develop sentence strips with sight words learnt over the unit. Take turns reading the sentences aloud to peers, emphasizing the targeted sight words. Review sentences and mount them in class. Read the combined set of sentences each morning.

**Spelling**
- In small groups, practise to spell grade level words relating to Integrated Studies and taken from the Grade level Reader and supplementary texts. Assist peers in using the relevant spelling/syllabication rules. With peer/teacher assistance, refer to online and other sources where they cannot readily recall rules.

**Vocabulary Development**
- Play the 'Tell Me the Word' game to review vocabulary learnt over the unit. (The leader uses word clue cards — word on one side, clues on the reverse — to give several clues to a word and a sentence to be completed using the word, while peers try to guess the word. E.g. *intelligent*: begins with 'i'; means 'can think through ideas quickly'; means 'can understand points easily'; synonym is 'smart'.)
- Play Pictionary: select a word from a 'Vocabulary Box'. A student draws pictures to give clues for the word so that peers can guess the word. If after three guesses the class is unable to determine the word, the 'artist' discloses the word and gives the meaning.
- In pairs, use assigned word categories to sort word cards, placing relevant words together — e.g. Feelings: *sadness, joy, thankfulness, regret, pity, anger*. Given a set of words (e.g. *baby, bear, boy, carrot, biscuit, bull, banana, cake*), group them in different ways — as animals, people, food, or words beginning with 'b'/'c'.

### Key Skills
Identify long vowel sounds · Blend sounds · Isolate/segment sounds · Read words/sentences with blends · Read r-controlled words · Apply syllabication rules · Apply the Word-Within-Word Strategy · Identify inflectional endings · Differentiate between words · Identify sight words · Take turns · Practise spelling rules · Use context clues · Determine antonym/synonym · Classify words

### Assessment
- Long vowel sounds in different word positions are accurately identified, properly pronounced and appropriately blended in order to recognize words.
- Consonant blends in the initial and final positions are accurately isolated, separated into phonemes and then blended together; words with blends in the final position are accurately read with adequate emphasis.
- R-controlled words are accurately read.
- Syllabication rules are appropriately revised and effectively applied in spelling/vocabulary activities; rules are appropriately determined to suit targeted words.
- The Word-Within-Word Strategy is effectively applied to decode targeted words.
- Inflectional endings accurately used to differentiate between word pairs.
- Sight words accurately identified in different reading contexts, places and sentences.
- Spelling/syllabication rules accurately applied to spell words; context clues effectively used to determine the meanings of unfamiliar words.
- Vocabulary accurately determined using game clues; words appropriately categorized and grouped to show connection.

### Resources / Points to Note
- Dolch Sight Word List for Grade 3; Fry's Word List (third hundred); words from Literacy 1-2-3 Big/Little books; words from Integrated Studies concepts; Syllabication Rule Kit; word cards; sentence strips; supplementary readers.

## Strand: Comprehension

### Attainment Targets
- Read for meaning, fluency and enjoyment of texts, using a variety of clues to gain information and identify ideas and events.
- Use deduction and inference to interpret information and ideas and to predict outcomes.
- Read fluently and with appreciation.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Openly talk about the strategies employed in deciphering meaning.
- Retell a story giving details about specific elements.
- Use context clues to clarify understanding.
- Demonstrate understanding of structure through the use of graphic organizers and outlines.
- Use self-questioning and teacher questioning to promote reading skills.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Select grade level text from the class library or reading corner. Work in pairs to read and understand the text. Rewrite the story and share with classmates. Collaborate with their teacher to assess and discuss the strategies used in retellings.
- Review specific elements of a story (character, setting, problem, solution) and present summaries on a graphic organizer. Listen to a recorded story or story read by peers and use graphic organizers to capture details relating to story elements. Retell the story to the class paying attention to the elements reviewed.
- Use the author's chair to recall and retell specific details from a story previously read or listened to, OR complete a book report template to retell details of a self-selected story.
- Observe as the teacher demonstrates using clues within the text to decipher the meaning of an unfamiliar word. Engage in teacher-led discussion to reflect on the model and identify components of the process.
- Complete a prepared/sourced worksheet requiring them to read riddles to find out the meaning of a make-believe word that is underlined in each sentence, and circle the correct meaning from a list. Then circle at least two clue words that helped them figure out the meaning.
- Work in pairs or small groups to read grade level texts. Identify sections of the text that provide clues to decipher the meanings of unfamiliar words. Extract and write those sentences or paragraphs on sticky note pads and share with the class.
- Work in small groups to play a game of "context clue detective". One group member is the detective at a time; other members call a word and page number, then search to identify clues that can aid understanding within a specified time. Use the clues to orally explain the meaning of the word or phrase. Take turns until everyone gets a chance.
- Discuss samples of questions that they may ask themselves before, during and after reading. Record questions in a table on chart/hand-out/whiteboard (columns: Before / During / After). Mount the chart in class for future reference.
- Examine and discuss samples of teacher-prepared questions that they can utilise during active reading.
- Observe as the teacher uses the think-aloud strategy to demonstrate the use of self-questioning during reading to monitor her understanding and progress. Students apply the strategy used by the teacher.
- Collaborate with their teacher to use the "Say Something" Strategy to monitor reading. Respond to the text using one of the stem starters outlined on the strategy poster.
- Work in pairs/small groups to select and read grade level text using questions drafted to monitor themselves and their peers before, during and after reading.
- Read a set of questions and work in pairs to read accompanying text and identify portions of the text that will aid them in responding to the questions asked. Participate in teacher-led discussion after the reading exercise.

### Key Skills
Rewrite story · Utilize story elements · Engage in discussion · Review story elements · Retell story · Recall story details · Observe teacher model · Identify examples of context clues · Use context clues · Discuss and write questions · Critique questions · Use self-questioning · Monitor own reading · Respond to text · Identify ideas in text

### Assessment
- Rewritten stories capture story details using the various story elements; discussions focus on the extent to which strategies are used to decipher meaning.
- Graphic organizers accurately capture explanations and examples of the story elements reviewed; oral retellings properly sequenced and aligned to the elements and the text read/recorded.
- Information shared through the author's chair / book report template reflects students' understanding and details extracted from the story.
- Discussions focus on reflecting on the teacher's process and highlighting the steps necessary in using context clues to decipher meaning.
- Worksheets completed with word meanings and clues accurately identified; context clues accurately identified, extracted and used to decipher the meaning of the word or phrase.
- Table completed with questions relevant to monitoring before, during and after reading; discussion highlights the nature and types of questions asked during active reading.
- Think-aloud strategy clearly demonstrates the processes involved in employing self-questioning during reading to monitor own reading.
- Responses reflect use of the stem starters in the "Say Something" Strategy and show direct alignment to the text read.
- Reading exemplifies active reading; questions effectively asked and responded to using information from text and experience.
- Portions of the text identified contain information relevant to answering the questions outlined.

## Strand: Grammar and Conventions

### Attainment Targets
- Write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated, using SJE and JC appropriately.
- Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between SJE and JC.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Use negative forms of the verbs "to be" and "to do", i.e. 'am not', 'is not', 'do not', 'did not', to write for a variety of purposes.
- Identify and use irregular plural nouns.
- Formulate and apply rules for the use of singular and plural demonstrative pronouns.
- Use the conjunction 'but' in a range of contexts to show the opposite or conflicting ideas.
- Use prepositions appropriately and explain their functions in relation to location and directions.
- Use a range of adjectives (oral and/or written) when describing size, shape, colour, texture.
- Practise the use of adjectives (regular) when comparing two nouns (-er) and more than two nouns (-est).
- Use adjectives that do not comply with the regular formation rules.
- Apply appropriate use of capital letters when writing.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Complete a teacher-made crossword puzzle where they find the plural form of irregular nouns. Create word cards with irregular plural nouns. In groups, match the singular and plural version of each noun, sort the cards and create a suitable label for each category based on the pluralization rule. Research other words for each category.
- Analyse given minimal pair sentences and explain the differences in meaning resulting from a change in the demonstrative pronoun. Record the rules for using given demonstrative pronouns — e.g. "I want this book." / "I want that book." / "May I look at those bags?" / "May I look at these bags?" Find examples of demonstrative pronouns in a range of texts and record what each pronoun is being used to represent. Use the same pronouns to create sentences of their own.
- Engage in a whole class/team debate on topical issues by countering the statement of a previous speaker using the phrases "Yes, but..." or "No, but...". Review sentences for the correct use of the conjunction 'but'. Revise sentences where the conjunction has been misused. Justify their decisions.
- In groups, write a simple story using 7-10 prepositions, leaving an empty space where a preposition should be. Exchange stories with another group that will complete the stories with appropriate prepositions. Explain their choices. Compete in groups to create a classroom preposition poster with illustrations depicting the meaning of common prepositions, and explain their posters to peers.
- Take an object to class hidden in a bag. Use appropriate adjectives to describe the object for peers in relation to shape, size, texture and colour. Have peers use the descriptions to guess the name of the item. Talk about why some adjectives may be more appropriate to describe some nouns.
- In teams of two, take turns to create positive and negative sentences. One member creates a positive sentence; the other counters by converting it into a negative sentence. Unscramble a given set of negative sentences. Expand each sentence by creating another related negative sentence.
- In small groups, use the comparative and superlative forms to write as many true statements comparing themselves with each other — e.g. "In our group Paul runs the fastest." Work in groups of three to create 'silly' three-sentence tales using a form of a given irregular adjective (e.g. *bad, worse, worst*) in each sentence.
- Edit narratives/letters/dialogues for accurate use of capital letters. Read stories and highlight the use of capital letters; justify the writer's use of capital letters. Create captions/slogans for popular manufacturing companies in their community applying appropriate capitalization rules.

### Key Skills
Complete crossword puzzle · Use irregular plural nouns · Match singular and plural nouns · Classify noun plurals · Analyse and compare sentences · Discuss meaning of demonstrative pronouns · Record rules · Use pronouns · Create sentences · Debate topical issues · Counter arguments · Review and revise sentences · Use the conjunction 'but' · Write stories · Use prepositions · Explain choices · Create preposition poster · Describe objects · Discuss adjective use · Create positive and negative sentences · Use comparative and superlative adjectives · Use irregular adjectives · Apply capitalization rules

### Assessment
- The plural form of irregular nouns appropriately used to solve the crossword puzzle; singular nouns accurately matched to their plural forms; irregular noun plurals correctly classified based on similar pluralization rule.
- Meaning associated with various demonstrative pronouns adequately discussed and recorded; examples of demonstrative pronouns correctly identified; pronouns satisfactorily used to construct sentences.
- Statements adequately countered using the conjunction 'but'; sentences satisfactorily reviewed and revised to reflect the correct use of 'but'; decisions adequately justified.
- Stories completed with appropriate use of a variety of prepositions; choices of prepositions adequately justified; posters satisfactorily use illustrations and are adequately explained.
- Objects adequately described using a range of adjectives; adjective use adequately discussed.
- Negative sentences accurately formed from positive statements, correctly unscrambled, and satisfactorily created to expand given sentences.
- Comparative and superlative adjectives appropriately used to compare students in groups; three-sentence tales satisfactorily created using all forms of given irregular adjectives.
- Narratives/letters/dialogues accurately edited for appropriate use of capital letters; writers' use of capital letters accurately justified; captions/slogans accurately reflect appropriate capitalization rules.

### Points to Note
- Negatives are formed by placing 'not' after the forms of "to be" and "to do" — e.g. "I am not going to cry."
- Some words with descriptive adjectives and hyphens form the plural by adding the correct affix to the noun portion — e.g. *father-in-law → fathers-in-law*; *mother-in-law → mothers-in-law*.
- Some animals have the same singular and plural form: *deer, moose, sheep, swine*.
- Demonstrative pronouns: Singular — *this* (near), *that* (far); Plural — *these* (near), *those* (far). 'Near' refers to near in distance or time (e.g. "This feels good."; "These are beautiful."); 'Far' refers to far in distance or time (e.g. "That is beautiful."; "Those are for you.").
- Capitalization applies to: brand names of manufacturers (Grace, Pepsi, Kraft); Roman numerals (e.g. XLV); and the first word of a direct quotation (e.g. "Stop!" father shouted.).

## Strand: Writing

### Attainment Targets
- Develop approaches to the writing process to enable them to organise their ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs.
- Write to narrate, to persuade and for a range of transactional purposes, using SJE and JC appropriately and incorporating multi-media approaches.
- Use language and text forms appropriately and with imagination to create vibrant and engaging texts.
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Write to compare different ideas/concepts.
- Record information learnt using own language.
- Use story maps to plan story.
- Write simple Language Experience Stories using ideas generated.
- With teacher support, review work done and improve as necessary.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Compare characters/ideas from different stories of similar genre. Give comparisons using graphic organizers or other illustrations.
- Listen to a presentation from a guest speaker. Make a journal entry describing what was learnt from the presentation.
- Use a completed story map (characters, setting, problem, ending) to complete a Story Plan graphic organizer.
- Observe as the teacher uses the think-aloud strategy to model writing Language Experience Stories (taking note of the use of direct speech). Engage in teacher-led discussion about the demonstration. Be randomly selected to model writing a language experience paragraph to share an experience/idea of their own.
- Collaborate to write a Language Experience Story about a recent event at their school using the shared writing strategy.
- Individually translate the story written in the shared writing activity to Standard Jamaican English (SJE). Share their revised stories with the class. Read and critique for accuracy and meaning.

### Key Skills
Compare characters/ideas · Give comparisons · Illustrate ideas · Record information · Complete story plans · Write paragraphs · Discuss demonstration · Generate ideas for writing · Compose experience stories · Translate to SJE · Share stories · Critique stories

### Assessment
- Comparisons satisfactorily made using graphic organizers/illustrations.
- Journals reflect correct information from the presentation.
- Story plans correctly capture information from the story map.
- Paragraphs adequately reflect use of the language experience approach.
- Shared writing strategy effectively used to compose Language Experience Stories.
- Stories correctly translated to SJE.
