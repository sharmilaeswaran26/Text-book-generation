# Grade 3 — Language Arts — Term 1 — Unit 2

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Language Arts, Term 1, Unit 2 (6 weeks). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Subject:** Language Arts
**Term:** 1
**Unit:** 2
**Duration:** 6 weeks

## Introduction to the Unit

In this unit, students continue to use the language skills and content across strands. There are opportunities for twinning strands. As students listen to Read Alouds, they identify main ideas and summarize stories while incorporating the structures of the language to present their summaries orally and in writing. The use of questioning is applied in a more refined manner as they use information from the text to form questions.

Students use the concepts and rules learnt in the language structure and apply them in a variety of authentic written and oral tasks. Students engage in the steps of the writing process with a focus on drafting and revising their writings. Their writings reflect more details and descriptive words. As students review and edit their writings they will participate in and appreciate peer assessment.

## Strand 1: Speaking and Listening

### Attainment Targets
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively.
- Listen to, recall, understand and respond to speakers' messages, whether implicit or explicit.
- Recognise, value and make distinctions between home language and SJE to improve/acquire language and literacy competences.
- Explain and comment on speakers' use of language, including use of SJE and JC, and their use of vocabulary, grammar and other features.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Explain their views/observations to small groups and to the class using SJE.
- Identify and sequence main ideas in a story.
- Paraphrase/summarize stories used in Read Aloud activities.
- Give, receive and act on information/instructions.
- Question the speaker to gain clarity or to confirm what is said.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Plan and deliver talk to various audiences (real or imagined). Participate in panel discussions or grand conversations. Describe and give opinions about observed activities in small groups. Some examples of items for discussion are: morning messages — current affairs, cultural events, persons, plants and animals in our community; Show and Tell — toys, family artefacts (photographs, jewellery).
- Listen to identify main ideas. Use puppets to retell how events occurred in a story they heard. Identify the main ideas in stories. Sequence the information heard by completing the following statements:
  - The first thing that happened was ________.
  - After that, ___________.
  - Then _______.
  - Finally, _______________.
- Paraphrase, summarize and recast. Repeat the information heard during class discussion or from a Read Aloud activity.
- Engage students to use their own words to share what they remember about a story that was shared. Give simple instructions in audible voice, e.g. "Stop writing. Close your book"; "Stand"; "Sit and listen attentively." Participate in games (e.g. Simon Says, 1-2-3 Red Light, Mother May I).
- Listen to a presentation from a class guest speaker. Write and ask questions to gain clarity about the presentation.

### Key Skills
Speak appropriately to different audiences · Give opinions · Describe activities · Recall and sequence information accurately · Identify main ideas · Paraphrase, summarize and recast information · Follow instructions accurately · Give clear instructions · Listen to presentation · Write questions · Ask questions

### Assessment
- Speech/talk/instructions are accurately structured and information is logically sequenced.
- Main ideas are identified and sequenced correctly.
- Stories are accurately paraphrased/summarized.
- Instructions given are followed correctly.
- Questions written or asked confirm or clarify given information.

## Strand 2: Word Recognition and Fluency

### Attainment Targets
- Read fluently and with appreciation.
- Automatically recognise words (including basic sight word lists) through repeated exposure and mnemonic devices.
- Establish a concept of print and use a range of word recognition clues, re-reading and reading ahead, to identify new words.
- Develop phonic awareness and use knowledge of letter-sound correspondences in order to decode unfamiliar words.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Blend consonant and vowel sounds to read grade level words.
- Read words with clusters in the initial and final positions.
- Read words with the 'r'-controlled vowels.
- Spell and practise correct pronunciation of the 'aw', 'au', 'augh', 'ough', 'a' vowels by applying appropriate articulation of the sounds, given the words in which the vowels are used.
- Spell and practise correct pronunciation of the 'ear', 'er', 'ar' vowels by applying appropriate articulation of the sounds, given the words in which the vowels are used.
- Revise and use syllabication rules to improve vocabulary and spelling.
- Apply the Word-within-Word strategy as a means of deciphering unknown words.
- Identify and explore the meaning of inflectional endings as a means of differentiating between words during the reading process.
- Read in isolation and in context, sight words appropriate to the grade level.
- Work cooperatively with peers to build vocabulary.
- Use context clues — pictures, words, sentences, paragraphs — as an aid to gain meaning of unfamiliar words.
- Use reference sources to confirm word meanings and appropriate use of targeted words in context.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- **Phonics:** In pairs, read portions of texts, decoding unfamiliar words by blending consonants and vowels. In pairs, sort cards with words containing clusters in the initial and final positions; identify the clusters and pronounce them clearly in each word; read sentences with these words, emphasizing the clusters. Become "Vowel Detectives": in small groups examine and compare two sets of vowels (e.g. *stick, send, scamp, hamburger, love*), then read words containing r-controlled vowels and determine the effect of the "Bossy r" (for example, /i/ in *stick* compared with /i/ in *stir*).
- **Sight Words:** Use games/jingles/poems/rhymes to review words on the Word Wall daily — individually and with peers — in order to build sight word vocabulary.
- **Structural Analysis:** Create posters to illustrate a syllabication rule (rule, an illustration and at least four examples of divided words). Apply the Word-within-Word strategy: cut grade level words from newspapers/magazines, list them, identify and cut out smaller words within the larger words, read the parts aloud, and work out the unknown words.
- **Inflectional Endings:** Search for pairs of words in class readers and supplementary texts that are differentiated by the presence/absence of inflectional endings. Share the meanings communicated by each word in the pair.
- **Spelling:** Watch videos or listen to tapes relating to the sounds 'aw', 'au', 'augh', 'ough', 'a' and practise their pronunciation using lists of related words; take turns assigning words to be spelt by peers. In pairs, select cards with 'ear', 'er' and 'ar' sounds, read them aloud, then close eyes and spell each word; take turns calling words while the partner spells.
- **Vocabulary Development:** Play the game "Figure it Out" with peers, using cards/strips containing word clues in pictures, words, sentences and paragraphs. One student rolls a die and selects a matching card; the goal is to supply the meaning of the word, and if they cannot, other students give context clues to assist. Participate in a dictionary drill activity to locate words quickly. Use a word from a list to complete a sentence, e.g. "joy / hoop / highway — Paul jumped for ___________ when he saw his new puppy."

### Key Skills
Blend phonemes · Produce sounds of clusters · Isolate and produce vowel sounds · Practise sight words · Create posters · Syllabicate words · Apply word recognition strategy · Analyse inflectional endings · Pronounce and spell vowel sounds · Use context clues · Locate word meanings from a dictionary

### Assessment
- Unfamiliar words are accurately blended and decoded.
- Clusters are accurately located and pronounced in targeted words.
- r-controlled vowels are accurately produced; impact of 'r' on vowels is satisfactorily explored.
- Sight words are decoded correctly.
- Rules, illustrations and division of words are correctly depicted on posters.
- The Word-within-Word strategy is effectively applied to decode unfamiliar words.
- Use of inflectional endings is appropriately analysed to determine how they impact word meaning and help to differentiate between words.
- Vowel sounds 'aw', 'au', 'augh', 'ough', 'a' and 'ear', 'er', 'ar' are accurately pronounced and spelt.
- Context clues are effectively used to determine word meaning; word meanings are accurately located in dictionaries; words are correctly used in sentences.

## Strand 3: Comprehension

### Attainment Targets
- Read for meaning, fluency and enjoyment of texts, using a variety of clues to gain information and identify ideas and events.
- Use deduction and inference to interpret information and ideas and to predict outcomes.
- Read fluently and with appreciation.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Make inferences before, during, and after reading.
- Identify main idea and supporting details.
- Clarify ideas through discussions and activities.
- Use information in text to form and refine questions and predictions.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Work in groups to review and discuss how to make inferences using a variety of inference charts or tips. Use the chart along with a familiar text to prepare two examples of making an inference. Complete a worksheet that requires them to read and use clues to decipher meaning, e.g. *"My father did not like to wear his crown. He said it was heavy and made his head hurt. While I did not have a crown, my parents made sure I had a silk dress with gold thread to wear for the coronation. Who Am I? ___________"* Use pictures, headings and sub-headings of selected texts to make inferences before, during and after reading, then comment on how this helped during the reading process.
- Engage in a collaborative activity to discuss tips that may be helpful when identifying main idea and supporting details in texts. Use the ideas to create a strategy poster for individual or whole-class use. Read grade level text and identify the main idea; select a sentence strip that appropriately captures the main idea. Read story excerpts/paragraphs/short stories and circle/underline the sentence(s) that indicate the main idea.
- Work in small groups to use the Readers Theatre strategy to summarise and present a story read; engage in a whole-class discussion to react to the presentation and clarify ideas. Work in pairs to write questions they would ask themselves to clarify ideas before, during and after reading; share questions with the class and use two or more during an oral reading session.
- Collaborate with the teacher to use the DRTA strategy to read grade level texts and decipher meaning. Use colour-coded sticky notes to write predictions and paste them at the point in the text where information supports or refutes the prediction; tick the note if aligned, or add a think bubble and modify the prediction if necessary. Work in pairs to peruse pictures, headings and subheadings, make predictions, write at least five questions, and read alternately to confirm or refute predictions. Engage in a discussion focused on making predictions about characters, setting and plot; record on charts; snap fingers when a portion of text supports or refutes a prediction, and discuss why.

### Key Skills
Apply strategy · Make inferences · Monitor own reading · Engage in discussion · Identify main idea · Identify supporting details · Summarise story · Clarify ideas · Write questions · Respond to questions · Read grade level text · Use the DRTA strategy · Refine predictions · Support or refute predictions with evidence · Cite evidence in texts

### Assessment
- Discussion focuses on how the chart guides the process of making an inference; sample inferences are accurately aligned to the text and demonstrate use of the steps outlined.
- Inferences made are accurate and aligned to the information provided.
- Discussion focuses on identifying and explaining helpful tips for identifying the main idea and supporting details; main ideas are accurately identified.
- The main idea sentence(s) are correctly identified.
- Readers Theatre presentation depicts a true summary of the text read; discussion provides an opportunity to seek clarification.
- Questions written sought to request or provide clarity; oral reading sessions demonstrate the use of questions to seek and provide clarification.
- Sticky notes are appropriately placed to confirm or refute ideas in the text with predictions made; predictions are modified in accordance with the information garnered from the text.
- Questions written are aligned with the information presented in pictures, headings and sub-headings; discussions concentrate on confirming and refuting predictions citing evidence from text.

## Strand 4: Grammar and Conventions

### Attainment Targets
- Write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated, using SJE and JC appropriately.
- Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between SJE and JC.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Use adjectives appropriately in written and oral tasks.
- Identify and use adverbs appropriately when composing sentences.
- Apply the concepts of adding -d, -ed to form the past tense.
- Use the irregular form rules of the simple past tense to build vocabulary and writing skills.
- Apply knowledge of pronouns (personal, possessive, demonstrative) in a variety of written and oral tasks.
- Use punctuation marks accurately (full stop, comma, and question mark) to compose simple sentences/paragraphs.
- Choose appropriate verbs to agree with he/she/it and other singular subjects.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Expand simple sentences by adding adjectives to make more interesting sentences. Discuss their choice and placement of adjectives in each sentence.
- On given word cards, half of the class will write an adjective while the other half writes a noun; move around the room to find a match, then partner to create a sentence using both. Search for adverbs in class readers or other materials and classify them into the categories WHEN? WHERE? HOW? TO WHAT EXTENT? Add an adverb to given simple sentences so that it satisfies the criterion in brackets, e.g. "The student answered the question. (HOW?)" → "The student CAREFULLY answered the question."
- Use the 3-2-1 strategy to discuss, in the simple past tense, stories read or classroom activities completed (3 — record three things you remember; 2 — record two things you liked; 1 — record one thing you did not like).
- In small groups, use teacher-made verb cards (with past tense verbs) to create simple stories written in the past tense.
- Use story starters as scaffolding, e.g. *"Late one night my neighbour knocked on the front door and asked...."* In small groups, use letter tiles to make pronouns and use them to make sentences. Read articles in a student newspaper (e.g. *Children's Own*), choose one or two paragraphs, underline all the nouns, replace them with pronouns, and discuss the impact on the writing.
- Edit given paragraphs for correct punctuation usage and rewrite using the correct punctuation marks. Compose post-card messages and discuss punctuation choices; with a partner, reflect on and explore other punctuation options.
- Design a subject/verb agreement poster (with examples) for the class display board, e.g. "He likes ...", "It is ...", "Jamaica produces ...". Complete conversations for a comic strip by using the correct verb.

### Key Skills
Use adjectives · Expand sentences · Justify adjective usage · Distinguish adjectives and nouns · Describe nouns · Construct sentences · Identify and use adverbs · Distinguish adverbs by function · Compose sentences · Use the past tense · Discuss past events · Work collaboratively · Create stories · Form and use pronouns · Identify and replace nouns · Discuss pronoun usage · Edit for punctuation · Use punctuation · Design poster · Present · Use correct subject/verb agreement · Design comic strip

### Assessment
- Adjectives are appropriately used to expand sentences; adjective choices and use are adequately justified.
- Adjectives and nouns are appropriately paired and used to construct sentences; adverbs are accurately identified and sorted based on function; sentences are appropriately expanded with a range of adverbs.
- Past tense forms of verbs are accurately used to discuss stories read or activities completed; past tense verbs are accurately used to create stories.
- Pronouns are accurately formed from letter tiles and appropriately used to create sentences; pronouns are appropriately used to replace nouns; pronoun usage in written pieces is meaningfully discussed.
- Given paragraphs are accurately edited and revised to reflect appropriate punctuation usage; punctuation choices are adequately discussed in written pieces.
- The subject/verb agreement poster adequately highlights subject/verb agreement rules and examples; sentences for the comic strip reflect accurate subject/verb agreement.

### Points to Note
- When using teacher-made verb cards to create stories in the simple past tense, attention should be given to irregular past tense forms (for example, *go → went*, *run → ran*, *eat → ate*).

## Strand 5: Writing

### Attainment Targets
- Develop approaches to the writing process to enable them to organise their ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs.
- Write to narrate, to persuade and for a range of transactional purposes, using SJE and JC appropriately and incorporating multi-media approaches to their writing.
- Use language and text forms appropriately and with imagination to create vibrant and engaging texts.
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Formulate interview questions using the 5 Ws — who, what, when, where, why.
- Summarize information or list critical/essential information.
- Develop headings for sets of simple sentences/paragraphs.
- Compose draft text using a paragraph frame.
- Extend generated words and phrases into sentences.
- Record details of an event that had or will happen (state event, what happened, to whom, when, where, why/how).
- Expand writing by adding details and descriptive words.
- With teacher support, review work done and improve as necessary.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Work in pairs to peruse a magazine, newspaper or text to identify questions that utilise the 5 Ws; write sample questions on a graphic organiser and mount it in the classroom. Observe as the teacher models writing questions with the 5 Ws by turning topics into questions, then engage in teacher-led discussion. Work with partners to write between five and ten questions to ask a resource person, then critique each other's work for accuracy.
- Engage in a teacher-led discussion/video focused on writing simple summaries. Listen to a short speech or presentation, then do a quick write to summarise the information heard. Work in pairs to read a teacher-prepared hand-out, identify main ideas, capture them on a graphic organiser, and use an interactive writing strategy to write a sample summary.
- Use sentence strips to write headings for a paragraph.
- Complete a paragraph frame, then use it to write a paragraph. Share paragraphs with an elbow partner and make suggestions for the next draft.
- Construct sentences with vocabulary words. Create a story/book incorporating all of their vocabulary words, where each sentence builds on the previous one and makes sense.
- Complete a graphic organiser about an activity that was planned for someone (Event/Activity; What happened?; To whom did it happen?; When?; Where?; Why/How?).
- Review the first draft of a piece of narrative writing; identify and rewrite sentences that can be improved by adding details/descriptive words. Use information from teacher response journals to review/rewrite various pieces of writing.

### Key Skills
Identify questions · Write questions with the 5 Ws · Observe teacher model · Engage in discussion · Construct questions · Practise critiquing · Do a quick write · Summarise text · Identify main idea · Write summary on a graphic organiser · Write headings · Compose paragraphs · Construct sentences · Create a story/book · Complete a graphic organiser · Record details · Review and rewrite drafts

### Assessment
- Graphic organiser is completed accurately with 5 Ws questions; questions accurately utilise the 5 Ws; critique is constructive and accurately aligned to information presented by peers.
- Discussion focuses on the processes involved in writing and critiquing summaries; main ideas are accurately identified and recorded; summary accurately captures and communicates main ideas and important supporting details.
- Headings are appropriately written and aligned to the paragraphs.
- Paragraph written is aligned to the paragraph frame; sentences are constructed correctly using vocabulary words; sentences in the story/book are adequately developed to make a cohesive piece of writing.
- Graphic organiser is completed satisfactorily to record details of an event.
- Rewritten draft reflects added details and use of descriptive words; drafts satisfactorily reflect evidence of teacher support.
