# Grade 3 — Language Arts — Term 2 — Unit 1

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Language Arts, Term 2, Unit 1 (6 weeks). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Subject:** Language Arts
**Term:** 2
**Unit:** 1
**Duration:** 6 weeks

## Introduction to the Unit

After completing one term in Grade Three, the students are now equipped with the necessary skills that will allow them to become more independent as learners. They will continue to apply previously learnt skills across the strands. In this unit, further opportunities are given for students to share opinions, to ask and answer questions using the listening and speaking skills.

There is emphasis on application of skills and concepts learnt that will better enable them to decode and encode words to increase fluency and understanding. They continue to use the writing process to express their ideas and utilise a variety of prewriting strategies in the selection of topics and organization of ideas. They write fully developed paragraphs that reflect essential paragraph elements. Writing checklists are used to edit documents for the traits of quality writing.

On completion of this unit, the students would have been exposed to activities that would have allowed them to scaffold on learnt skills and concepts that will greatly impact the development of the language skills.

## Strand 1: Speaking and Listening

### Attainment Targets
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively.
- Listen to, recall, understand and respond to speakers' messages, whether implicit or explicit.
- Recognise, value and make distinctions between home language and SJE to improve/acquire language and literacy competences.
- Explain and comment on speakers' use of language, including use of SJE and JC, and their use of vocabulary, grammar and other features.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Plan and deliver a speech to an audience
- Ask and answer questions to gain/clarify information/instructions
- Listen and follow instructions accurately
- Listen to story summaries then respond, sharing opinions and interpretations of the same story

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- In groups, use given roles (e.g. Member of Parliament, principal, famous sports personalities, artistes) to discuss, plan and deliver a Speech, Introduction or Vote of Thanks to a named audience. Negotiate roles for planning and delivering the Speech, Introduction and Vote of Thanks.
- In pairs, students will be given pictures and plain paper. The student with the picture will give instructions to the other student that will allow him to draw the picture. The student who is drawing must not be shown the picture until he has finished drawing.
- View safety signs and posters. The leader throws a ball to a student and asks a question about a sign or poster. The student catches the ball and responds to the question. The student then throws the ball to another student and asks a related question. The activity continues until students are unable to think of questions or respond to questions about a sign/poster. Another sign/poster is used to generate new responses and questions.
- After listening to readings/recordings of stories, students will work in groups to develop a summary of the story. Groups will present their summaries. Students compare and contrast summaries.

### Key Skills
Speak appreciatively · Assign roles · Give clear instructions · Follow instructions · Listen attentively · Respond to questions · Ask questions · Listen responsively · Share opinions · Interpret ideas

### Assessment
- Words used appropriately; demonstrated appreciation of others
- Pictures drawn reflect that students followed instructions satisfactorily
- Relevant questions asked and answered satisfactorily
- Summaries adequately reflect central and main ideas of the story

### Points to Note
- The drawing activity can also be done as a whole-group activity where the teacher gives the instruction. Pictures can be rotated so that the roles are reversed.

## Strand 2: Word Recognition and Fluency

### Attainment Targets
- Read fluently and with appreciation.
- Automatically recognise words (including basic sight word lists) through repeated exposure and mnemonic devices.
- Establish a concept of print and use a range of word recognition clues, re-reading and reading ahead, to identify new words.
- Develop phonic awareness and use knowledge of letter-sound correspondences in order to decode unfamiliar words.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Identify and accurately pronounce vowel digraphs and diphthongs in isolation and in context
- Read words with the 'r'-controlled vowels
- Revise and use syllabication rules to improve vocabulary and spelling
- Use knowledge of inflectional endings to read and decipher meaning of unfamiliar words
- Read in isolation and in context, sight words appropriate to the grade level
- Work cooperatively with peers to build vocabulary
- Use context clues — pictures, words, sentences, paragraphs — as an aid to gain meaning of unfamiliar words
- Use reference sources to confirm word meanings and appropriate use of targeted words in context
- Begin to use the knowledge of roots and affixes to determine the meanings of words
- Substitute words in context to reflect similar and different meanings through practice with synonyms and antonyms
- Practise the appropriate use of homonyms and homographs

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:

**Phonics**
- *Dip and Read:* Take turns to pull sentence strips from a bag and read aloud sentences, emphasizing vowel digraphs and diphthongs.
- In pairs, read selected portions of grade level texts or other material containing words with r-controlled vowels. Listen to each other and give feedback on how r-controlled vowels are pronounced in the context of words/sentences.

**Structural Analysis**
- Spell words aloud, clearly demonstrating the application of syllabication rules; for example: the word *cycle* is spelt showing that each syllable has a vowel sound — cy-cle; the word *shallow* is spelt splitting the syllables between the double consonants — shal-low.
- In small groups, compare sets of related words and indicate shifts in meaning arising from the presence of inflectional endings in some words, e.g. position / positioned; believe / believing.

**Sight Words**
- Using charts provided in different spaces and working in small groups, take turns in reading aloud different sight words indicated.
- Individually, select three (3) sight words from the list and use each to write a sentence. Exchange and read sentences aloud, emphasizing the sight word in each case.

**Vocabulary Development**
- Play the game 'Open your Eyes.' In groups, work to find the meanings of three (3) unfamiliar words using a set of different clues — pictures, words, sentences and paragraphs. Use these clues to confirm that the determined meaning is likely to be accurate. The first group that is able to accurately work out the meanings of the targeted words will be declared the winner.
- Complete a crossword puzzle. Students will use reference sources to help them in completing the puzzle. Use vocabulary words to make sentences.
- Match index cards with affixes to root words after the definition is read aloud, e.g. *care / ful / less* — Meaning: showing much thought and attention. Use the word in a sentence.
- Use the synonyms and/or antonyms of given words to make/complete sentences. Play the *Snap* card game: select 10 words; for each word select 2 synonyms and 2 antonyms; words are written on the cards; in groups of six, each player is given three cards and the rest stays in the deck. One card is faced up. If a player has a synonym or antonym of the word, they throw down the matched card. If another player can match the card, he does the same. If the card cannot be matched, then another card is drawn from the deck. The player who disposes of all the cards first wins.
- Complete a story/passage using given homonyms and homographs. In pairs, select a homograph from a container. One student uses it to make a sentence and the other student uses the homograph in a different context.

### Key Skills
Read vowel digraphs/diphthongs · Pronounce r-controlled vowels · Apply syllabication rules · Analyse words · Decipher word meanings · Read sight words · Use different word clues · Determine word meanings · Use reference materials · Make sentences · Match word and meanings · Use affixes · Create sentences · Substitute synonyms · Match synonyms and antonyms · Select homonyms and homographs · Practise using homographs

### Assessment
- Vowel digraphs/diphthongs accurately read in sentences
- r-controlled vowels accurately pronounced in the context of words/sentences
- Syllabication rules appropriately applied to spell words
- Knowledge/understanding of the use of inflectional endings effectively used to determine the meanings of unfamiliar words
- Sight words accurately read in isolation and in context
- Word meanings accurately determined based on the effective use of context clues
- Crossword puzzle completed satisfactorily; vocabulary words used appropriately in sentences
- Affixes correctly matched to root words; words appropriately used in sentences
- Synonyms correctly substituted in context; synonyms and/or antonyms are paired correctly
- Homonyms and homographs selected correctly

## Strand 3: Comprehension

### Attainment Targets
- Read for meaning, fluency and enjoyment of texts, using a variety of clues to gain information and identify ideas and events.
- Use deduction and inference to interpret information and ideas and to predict outcomes.
- Read fluently and with appreciation.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Willingly contribute to class discussions and decision before, during and after the reading process
- Make inferences and reasonable predictions
- Use information in text to modify predictions and questions
- Use main idea and supporting details to summarize text

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Work in small groups to review a making-inference strategy poster. Use an example from a text or picture to discuss the strategy poster. Complete teacher-prepared Making Inference Worksheets based on grade level text read, recording inferences made and the reasons for them.
- Use pictures to predict the next event in a story. As the story is read, the students will view a picture and make their predictions. After the section is read, students will confirm or refute their predictions.
- Engage in a teacher-led discussion focused on making and modifying predictions as well as asking and answering questions during reading (the teacher may use a teacher-prepared/sourced chart with tips and steps to guide the discussion).
- Engage in the use of the Directed Reading Thinking Activity (DRTA) to develop the skill of making and modifying predictions. Read to pre-determine stopping points, then pause to confirm, refute or modify predictions made before and during reading.
- Engage in a teacher-led discussion focused on what is a main idea and what are supporting details. Read/listen to grade level text, then complete a Summary Graphic Organizer using information from the text.

### Key Skills
Review making inference · Make inferences · Make predictions · Engage in discussion · Modify predictions · Confirm predictions · Refute predictions · Use main idea and supporting details · Summarize text

### Assessment
- Discussions are focused on utilizing the strategy poster during reading; examples cited are accurate; worksheets are accurately completed with reasonable inferences and supporting evidence
- Predictions appropriately confirmed or refuted
- Discussions are focused on the necessary tips and steps to be taken when making and modifying predictions as well as asking and answering questions during reading
- Predictions are made, confirmed, refuted and modified based on evidence from text and experience
- Discussions are focused on establishing a clear understanding of main idea and supporting details
- Graphic organizers completed with accurate information from text

## Strand 4: Grammar and Conventions

### Attainment Targets
- Write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated, using SJE and JC appropriately.
- Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between SJE and JC.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Identify and use proper nouns in a variety of texts
- Identify and use common nouns
- Apply the distinction of gender when using nouns
- Use full stops and commas when writing simple sentences
- Show appropriate use of the apostrophe (') when writing singular possessive nouns
- Construct sentences (orally or written) demonstrating the use of subject-verb agreement
- Apply basic past tense rules in speech and written work (add -d and -ed)
- Apply the concept of the regular form (-ied) of the past tense to build vocabulary and writing skills
- Identify and use the concept of the past participle to build writing skills
- Begin to extend the way they use adverbs in written work

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Participate in a Proper Noun 'Gallery Walk' where they will move around the classroom in groups to write examples of proper nouns on charts. Charts may be given various headings, e.g. Names of Girls, Schools, Important Events, etc. (Groups may compete to see how many proper nouns they can generate in a given time.) Pick teacher-made proper noun cards from a basket and use them to create sentences/short paragraphs.
- Compete in teams to see how many common nouns they can generate from given categories, e.g. community workers, fruits, places, etc. Complete story frames/poems by filling in the blank spaces with appropriate common nouns. Share product with class.
- Collect pictures to create Noun Gender Albums. Use the following categories: Feminine, Masculine, Common, Neuter. Complete sentences with the correct gender nouns, e.g. *She and her _______ are celebrating their 50th wedding anniversary.*
- Complete online or teacher-sourced worksheets practising the use of the full stop and the comma in simple sentences. Use a punctuation editing wheel (a wheel with the relevant punctuation rules) to edit the sentences of their peers for full stops and commas.
- Change different expressions into the possessive case, e.g. *the shirt of the boy*; *the punishment of a thief*. Create signs for their classroom/school using singular possessive nouns.
- View a teacher-prepared/online Web Quest to understand the concept of subject/verb agreement. Circle the subject and underline the verb in given simple sentences. Create lists of singular/plural subjects and verbs. Work in small groups to write a short story/paragraph, remove a few of the subjects and verbs, exchange with another group and have them provide singular or plural subjects and verbs to complete the story/paragraph. Share their stories/paragraphs with the class.
- Use a given list of verbs to complete a dialogue in the simple past tense. Compete to speak on a given topic for 30 seconds using the simple past tense. (Students are out of the game if they stop talking before the given time or if they do not use the past tense.)
- Match regular verbs to their past tense forms, e.g. a verb ending with the consonant 'y' — remove the 'y' and add 'ied' — *try → tried.*
- Complete teacher-made/online crossword puzzles using the past participle of verbs. Complete given sentences using the past participle form of the verb. Listen to news items being read and identify and record the use of the verbs in the past participle. Complete a table inserting the past and present tense of the past participle identified (e.g. play / played; sing / sang / sung; cut / cut / cut).
- Examine the use of adverbs in a variety of texts. Make a note of the adverbs and the words they are being used to modify. Determine whether the word is a VERB or ADJECTIVE. Talk about the impact of the adverbs on the meaning of the sentence. Use adverbs in a range of texts to enhance meaning.

### Key Skills
Write examples of proper nouns · Construct sentences/paragraphs · Use proper nouns · Work collaboratively · Generate common nouns · Complete stories/poems · Collect pictures · Classify nouns · Use gender nouns · Use full stops and commas · Create and edit sentences · Write the singular possessive form of nouns · Create signs · View for information · Identify subject and verb · Classify subjects · Use singular/plural subjects and verbs · Complete dialogue · Use the simple past tense · Speak freely · Form the past tense · Solve crossword puzzles · Identify past participle forms · Complete sentences · Listen for information · Record information · Discuss adverb use · Use adverbs

### Assessment
- Charts accurately completed with examples of proper nouns; sentences/paragraphs appropriately reflect use of proper nouns
- Common nouns appropriately generated under given categories; appropriate common nouns used to complete story frames/poems
- Pictures of nouns accurately classified according to gender; sentences appropriately completed with gender nouns
- Full stops and commas accurately used to complete exercises on worksheets; sentences adequately edited for correct use of full stops and commas
- The singular possessive form accurately formed from given expressions; the singular possessive form correctly used to create classroom/school signs
- Subject and verb accurately identified; singular and plural subjects and verbs correctly identified and classified; stories/paragraphs completed with accurate subject/verb agreement
- Given verbs appropriately used to complete the dialogue in the simple past tense; speech on given topic satisfactorily delivered using the simple past tense
- Verbs correctly matched to their past tense forms
- Crossword puzzles correctly completed using the past participle; past participle verb forms correctly identified and recorded
- The use and impact of adverbs are adequately discussed; adverbs appropriately used to enhance the meaning of texts

## Strand 5: Writing

### Attainment Targets
- Develop approaches to the writing process to enable them to organise their ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs.
- Write to narrate, to persuade and for a range of transactional purposes, using SJE and JC appropriately and incorporating multi-media approaches to their writing.
- Use language and text forms appropriately and with imagination to create vibrant and engaging texts.
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Formulate simple interview questions
- Pose formulated questions
- Record information gleaned
- Use a variety of pre-writing strategies (e.g. webbing, brainstorming, listing, note taking, outlining, drafting, graphic organizers) to choose a topic and organize ideas
- Write fully developed paragraphs using proper form (e.g. topic sentence, details, summary, conclusion sentence)
- Revise and use an editing checklist to edit documents for the traits of quality writing (e.g. ideas, organization, word choice, voice, sentence fluency, conventions and presentation)
- Prepare and publish final drafts of informative paragraphs

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:

**Writing to Inform — Interview**
- Work collaboratively with the teacher to develop interview questions based on a topic of interest using the 5 Ws fingers: who, what, when, where, why.
- Work in pairs to conduct interviews with peers using the questionnaire developed. Record interview notes/responses in an interview log.

**Pre-writing Activities**
- Listen to a portion of a grade level informational text read by the teacher. Respond to teacher-prepared questions geared at soliciting and highlighting the elements of informational texts (e.g. factual information, etc.).
- Use a semantic map to record facts related to a topic of choice. Discuss the information on the map within their groups.
- Work in groups to conduct a data collection drive to gather factual information to support their writing (e.g. capture pictures, observations, note details). Create a folder to store all the materials/evidence/data collected from the data collection drive.

**Drafting Activities**
- Compose draft texts using information from the semantic map and data folder using a paragraph frame, e.g. Author's Name; Informative Writing; Title; Topic Sentence; Fact 1; Fact 2; Fact 3; Fact 4.

**Revising Activities**
- Engage in a game of "I Spy." Each group member will peruse a copy of the piece under scrutiny to search for errors re: Capitalization, Organisation, Punctuation and Spelling (COPS). Call "I Spy" when an error has been identified, then state the error and where it can be found.
- Use a COPS revision checklist to review their drafts to identify elements of their writing that need improvement, using feedback from the teacher and peers, with a focus on specific features (e.g. a strong opening or "lead"; clarity of the main idea and inclusion of facts to support the topic).

**Editing and Proofreading Activities**
- Work in small groups to use an editing wheel to edit each other's work. Spin the wheel and use the prompt at the opening to edit the writing.

**Publishing**
- Engage in using the publishing strategy "3 Compliments and a Wish" — read aloud their written work to the class. Then call on 3 students to give 3 compliments and 1 student to give 1 wish based on the piece read.
- Write completed written work on newsprint/construction paper to be mounted on the school's bulletin board.

### Key Skills
Formulate interview questions · Conduct interview · Record interview notes · Generate ideas · Record ideas on topic · Discuss information · Collect data · Compose sentences relating to topic · Revise work · Develop checklist · Use checklist to edit writing · Publish written work

### Assessment
- Questionnaire highlights the use of the 5 Ws: who, what, when, where, why; questions reflect alignment with topic of choice
- Interviews demonstrate good composition of questions using the 5 Ws; interview log reflects an understanding of good note-taking technique
- Ideas generated and recorded demonstrate alignment with topic; data collected satisfactorily supports writing
- Sentences provide a clear topic sentence, supporting details and evidence to support statements
- Revised writing reflects satisfactorily feedback from peers and teacher, focusing on topic sentence, supporting details, sequencing of ideas, etc.
- Checklist developed includes the areas of focus: topic sentence, supporting details, sequencing of ideas, etc.
- Published texts show good use of all the elements learnt: topic sentence, supporting details, sequencing of ideas, etc.
