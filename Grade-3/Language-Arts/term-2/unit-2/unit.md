# Grade 3 — Language Arts — Term 2 — Unit 2

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3 Language Arts, Term 2, Unit 2 (6 weeks). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Subject:** Language Arts
**Term:** 2
**Unit:** 2
**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Strands:** Speaking and Listening · Word Recognition and Fluency · Comprehension · Grammar and Conventions · Writing

---

## Introduction to the Unit

> Students engage in learning the language skills and content through scaffolding. They engage in the learning process by participating in activities that will allow them to listen, speak, read and write. In this unit more practise is given to the skills across the strands. The skills are reinforced in innovative ways.
>
> In this unit, new concepts are introduced and students are given opportunities to apply their newly acquired content and skills. As students read, they use their personal experiences to critique texts. They apply the rules of specific mechanics and grammar in their writings. Students use appropriate formats for writing. In this unit, emphasis is placed on the reviewing and revising stages of the writing process.
>
> At the end of this unit, students would have demonstrated satisfactory application of Language Arts content and skills.

## Strand: Speaking and Listening

### Attainment Targets

> - Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively
> - Listen to, recall, understand and respond to speakers' messages, whether implicit or explicit
> - Recognise, value and make distinctions between home language and SJE to improve/acquire language and literacy competencies
> - Explain and comment on speakers' use of language, including use of SJE and JC, and their use of vocabulary, grammar and other features

### Objectives

> Students should be able to:
> - Communicate in different contexts and through various media for a variety of purposes
> - Ask and answer questions about information received
> - Respond to story summaries of classmates
> - Talk about how they feel about an event and characters in the story
> - Use answers/questions to reflect on the actions taken in response to various instructions/information

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities

> Students will:
> - Retell main ideas or events in a video/electronic story/PowerPoint presentation on communication.
> - Discuss means of communication — e.g. mail, texting, telephone, Money Gram, FedEx, Wire transfer, letters, Skype, Tango and Whatsapp.
> - Discuss: How am I alike/different from the people I communicate with outside of Jamaica? (Race, religion, culture, education)
> - Plan Talks: Ask questions to generate ideas. Practice questioning techniques with classmates. Practice reporting information received.
> - Play the "What if" game in small groups. One student will ask "What if..." and the other students would answer or pose other questions. The class reflects on the responses (Does it make sense? Could it happen?).
> - View films/recordings about stories depicting our culture. Select and give their opinions about a character or an event in the story.
> - Present summaries after viewing/listening. Discuss if summaries presented had relevant information.

### Key Skills

> Listen attentively · Recall and retell details accurately · Compare and contrast effectively · Generate questions · Respond to questions · Reflect on responses · Summarise stories · Give opinions

### Assessment

> - Main ideas/facts appropriately identified and discussed
> - Questions generated adequately addressed the discussion topics
> - Responses to questions related satisfactorily
> - Oral summaries accurately reflected information received
> - Responses to summaries adequately supported or refuted based on evidence

## Strand: Word Recognition and Fluency

### Attainment Targets

> - Read fluently and with appreciation
> - Automatically recognise words (including basic sight word lists) through repeated exposure and mnemonic devices
> - Establish a concept of print and use a range of word recognition clues, re-reading and reading ahead, to identify new words
> - Develop phonic awareness and use knowledge of letter-sound correspondences in order to decode unfamiliar words

### Objectives

> Students should be able to:
> - Produce the different sounds associated with targeted graphemes
> - Read words with vowel digraphs and diphthongs in context
> - Revise and use syllabication rules to improve vocabulary and spelling
> - Use knowledge of inflectional ending to read and decipher meaning of unfamiliar words
> - Read in isolation and in context, sight words appropriate to the grade level
> - Spell words using their understanding of the relationship between segmenting & blending phonemes and blending graphemes
> - Develop or use existing mnemonics to learn to spell tricky words or words with irregular grapho-phonic relationships
> - Use different strategies to learn to spell tricky words or words with irregular grapho-phonic relationships
> - Work cooperatively with peers to build vocabulary
> - Use context clues, pictures, words, sentences, paragraphs as an aid to gain meaning of unfamiliar words
> - Use reference sources to confirm word meanings and appropriate use of targeted words in context
> - Begin to use the knowledge of roots and affixes to determine the meanings of words
> - Substitute words in context to reflect similar and different meanings through practice with synonyms and antonyms
> - Practise the appropriate use of homonyms and homographs

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities

> Students will:
>
> **Phonics**
> - Read a passage/verse/jingle that has the targeted grapheme repeated many times. Identify the words that have the targeted grapheme, repeat the sound and give other words that have the targeted grapheme.
> - Make up three sentences with words that have diphthongs and digraphs. In pairs, read each other's sentences, check each other's work and make corrections where needed. e.g. *Joy has a toy that she will give to Roy.* / *The man put the soil on the foil.*
> - Complete a cloze passage with words from the list. e.g. (words: moist, enjoy, choice, toys) — *Although it rained we did ______ our trip to the beach. The sand was ______ after the rain. We played with our ______ instead of going into the water. I think we made the right ______.*
>
> **Structural Analysis**
> - Arrange themselves in a circle. The teacher throws a ball to a student and calls out a word. The student catches the ball, breaks the word into syllables and spells the word. Use the words from the game to make sentences.
> - Using picture clues, work in pairs to select the best word to complete sentences. e.g. *long, longer, longest* — *The snake is the ______ animal.* / *shelf, shelves* — *There were many books on the ______.*
>
> - Participate in a word game with a list of targeted sight words. Teams select the correct sight word to complete a displayed sentence to win a point. Use the paired reading strategy to read a passage that contains the sight words from the list.
>
> **Spelling**
> - Participate in a class "Spell Off" to spell two- and three-syllable words.
> - Use online and offline resources to research 2 mnemonics for spelling difficult words. Share with the class. Create a class book of mnemonics for spelling difficult words.
> - Identify and share different strategies used for spelling tricky words. In groups, create strategy bookmarkers. Apply these strategies to spell tricky words and report to class whether they were helpful.
>
> **Vocabulary**
> - Work in groups to create a "Vocabulary Playlist." Given the vocabulary words, think of a song that has that word and make a connection with the word as it is used in the song. (e.g. *twinkle* — "Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star" — the word is used to describe the star.)
> - Complete a rebus story activity sheet.
> - Read sentences. Select the meaning, then identify the words that helped to give the meaning of the highlighted word. e.g. *She penned a letter to her aunt.* (a. took  b. wrote)
> - Use thesaurus and dictionary to find the meanings of targeted words to complete worksheets.
> - Play an affixes domino game. As students match root words to affixes to make new words, they should make a sentence with the word that gives its meaning. e.g. *His careless behaviour caused the accident.*
> - Complete sentences by giving the synonym/antonym of a given word. Write the antonym/synonym of the word in brackets to complete sentences. e.g. *The ______ man gave all his money away.* (rich)
> - Complete a table by writing a sentence that correctly gives the meaning of the words as used in context (homonyms/homographs). e.g. *back* (part of the human body); *back* (opposite of front).

### Key Skills

> Identify and reproduce targeted grapheme · Create, read and edit sentences · Complete cloze passage · Syllabicate words · Construct sentences · Spell words · Identify picture clues · Select words · Select and read sight words · Segment phonemes · Blend graphemes · Research information · Create class book · Identify, share and apply strategies · Cooperate in groups · Share ideas · Identify song titles · Identify context clues · Select meanings · Use dictionaries and thesaurus · Match affixes to root words · Give synonyms/antonyms

### Assessment

> - Targeted grapheme correctly identified and reproduced
> - Sentences created, read and edited correctly; cloze passage completed accurately using given words
> - Words correctly separated into syllables; words correctly spelt
> - Words selected correctly to complete sentences; sight words correctly read in passages
> - Words spelt correctly; mnemonics appropriately used to spell tricky words; class book created
> - Strategies appropriately identified, shared and used to spell tricky words
> - Vocabulary playlist satisfactorily created to show connections with the word and the song
> - Vocabulary word correctly identified using context clues; worksheets satisfactorily completed
> - Affixes correctly matched to root words; sentences constructed to reflect meanings
> - Sentences completed correctly with appropriate synonyms/antonyms
> - Sentences constructed satisfactorily to give the suggested meaning (homonyms/homographs)

### Points to Note

> - Students can make up the rules for the games. They can also suggest words for the list of words to spell.

## Strand: Comprehension

### Attainment Targets

> - Read for meaning, fluency and enjoyment of texts, using a variety of clues to gain information and identify ideas and events
> - Use deduction and inference to interpret information and ideas and to predict outcomes
> - Read fluently and with appreciation

### Objectives

> Students should be able to:
> - Share personal experiences that help them to connect with and understand written work
> - Critique text using personal experiences
> - Identify elements of poetry to enhance understanding
> - Discuss meaning in poetry

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities

> Students will:
> - Engage in a teacher-led brainstorming activity to discuss the meaning and intentions of a critique. (What does it mean to critique?) Observe as the teacher demonstrates using his/her own experiences to critique a text.
> - Write in journals about a personal experience that is related to the read text. Share the journal entry with an elbow partner.
> - Read grade-level text and work in pairs to use personal experiences to critique the text based on guiding questions/focus areas given by the teacher (these may be displayed on a chart for reference).
> - Work in groups to define/explain terms (rhythm, rhymes, stanzas, imagery, etc.) previously learnt. Engage in a whole-class discussion focused on establishing correct understanding of the terms that highlight basic poetic devices.
> - Listen as the teacher reads a piece of text that has rhymes and a rhythm. State whether or not the text read is a poem and what caused them to decide that it was or was not.
> - View a teacher-prepared/sourced PowerPoint presentation focused on discussing imagery, mood and theme in poetry. Engage in a whole-class discussion relating to the presentation viewed.
> - Observe as teacher models using his/her knowledge of imagery, mood and themes in poetry to extract/establish meaning from a poem.
> - Listen as teacher reads grade-level poem aloud. Engage in a discussion to identify the lines that create imagery. Work in small groups to insert this information in a 5 Senses graphic organizer. Present the graphic organizers to the class and state how these helped them to understand how the poet is feeling as well as how they felt after reading the poem.
> - Work in pairs/small groups to read a grade-level poem issued by the teacher and use emotion stickers to identify different emotions/moods identified in the poem (e.g. a student will paste a sad face sticker at a line/stanza that caused them to feel sad or believe that the author is sad). Make an oral presentation to the class.

### Key Skills

> Engage in discussion · Observe teacher demonstration · Write journal entries · Share journals · Critique texts · Use guiding questions · Review poetic devices · Identify poetic devices · Identify imagery · Discuss meaning · Detect mood/feelings

### Assessment

> - Discussions focused on explaining the meaning and intentions of a critique
> - Journal entries depicted logical connections with personal experiences and texts
> - Critiques were aligned to the guiding questions/focus areas given by the teacher and were based on the students' personal experiences
> - Discussions focused on accurately explaining the terms related to the poetic devices
> - Responses confirmed that the piece read is a poem as well as highlighted the poetic devices employed
> - Discussions focused on establishing an understanding of how imagery, mood and themes provide/suggest meaning in poetry
> - Graphic organizers accurately completed and highlighted students' understanding of imagery in poetry
> - Mood/emotions identified are appropriately conveyed in the poem; oral presentations focused on highlighting students' reasons/rationale for the decisions they took

## Strand: Grammar and Conventions

### Attainment Targets

> - Write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated, using SJE and JC appropriately
> - Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between SJE and JC

### Objectives

> Students should be able to:
> - Use full stops and commas when writing sentences and paragraphs. Use the apostrophe ( ' ) to show singular possession
> - Demonstrate knowledge of the various forms of the verb, i.e. present, past and past participle (regular and irregular)
> - Use the negative forms of a verb in a simple sentence (present and past tense)
> - Use question marks to construct questions with 'do', 'who', 'where', 'which', 'when'
> - Apply the use of the apostrophe to form contractions
> - Practice using comparative adjectives (irregular)
> - Appropriately use the conjunction 'but' in written and oral work
> - Begin to use conjunctions such as 'while', 'when', 'where' to expand sentences
> - Use comparative adjectives to make unequal comparisons, i.e. adjective + '-er' 'than'
> - Compare adjectives using the phrase 'more...than'

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities

> Students will:
> - Complete given sentences by adding a singular possessive noun. e.g. *Mark drove ______ car to the store.* Combine simple sentences using the singular possessive noun. e.g. *The bag was locked in the car. It belonged to my teacher.* → *My teacher's bag was locked in the car.*
> - Rewrite sentences/paragraphs using the correct punctuation marks (full stops and commas).
> - Complete various texts using the correct verb tense — e.g. letters, dialogue, comic strip conversations.
> - Convert given positive sentences into negative forms. e.g. *I clean my classroom.* → *I do not clean my classroom.* / *I cleaned my classroom.* → *I did not clean my classroom.* Participate in a puppet show where they provide answers to questions.
> - Complete given sentences by putting in appropriate question words and question mark. e.g. *_____ is your birthday__* Construct questions to gather data on given topics.
> - Rewrite a teacher-sourced poem by replacing the underlined expressions with contractions. e.g. *I am → I'm; did not → didn't.*
> - Bring two objects to class (e.g. teddy bears, trucks, books). Describe the objects for peers using comparative adjectives. Record their favourite description and highlight the comparative adjectives used. In groups, identify and correct errors in the use of comparative adjectives.
> - Join simple sentences using the conjunction 'but'.
> - Complete sentence frames by using comparative adjectives.
> - Collect pictures of items in teacher-selected categories (e.g. homes, cars, plants). Work in groups to compare pictures from the same category using the words 'more...than'. e.g. *Nadine's house is more beautiful than Gary's.* Record the sentences produced and the rule governing comparing these adjectives.

### Key Skills

> Complete sentences · Use singular possessive nouns · Combine sentences · Punctuate sentences/paragraphs · Use correct verb tense · Construct negative sentences · Manipulate puppets · Complete questions · Use question mark · Construct questions · Use contractions · Compare nouns · Identify and use comparative adjectives · Record information · Identify and correct errors · Join sentences · Use conjunction · Complete sentence frames · Work collaboratively · Compare items

### Assessment

> - Sentences accurately completed using singular possessive nouns; sentences appropriately combined using singular possessive nouns
> - Sentences/paragraphs satisfactorily rewritten with the correct punctuation marks
> - Correct verb tense used appropriately to complete a range of texts
> - Positive sentences correctly converted to negative sentences
> - Question words and question mark used appropriately to complete questions; questions appropriately constructed based on given topics
> - Contractions appropriately formed and used to replace underlined expressions
> - Comparative adjectives appropriately used to compare objects; descriptions satisfactorily recorded and comparative adjectives accurately highlighted; errors correctly identified and corrected
> - Conjunction appropriately used to connect simple sentences
> - Sentence frames appropriately completed using appropriate comparative adjectives
> - Items in pictures adequately compared using the relevant comparative adjectives accurately

## Strand: Writing

### Attainment Targets

> - Develop approaches to the writing process to enable them to organise their ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs
> - Write to narrate, to persuade and for a range of transactional purposes, using SJE and JC appropriately and incorporating multi-media approaches to their writing
> - Use language and text forms appropriately and with imagination to create vibrant and engaging texts
> - Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them

### Objectives

> Students should be able to:
> - Use a variety of pre-writing strategies (e.g. webbing, brainstorming, listing, outlining, drafting, graphic organizers) to choose a topic and organise ideas
> - Identify and use parts of a friendly letter to generate ideas for writing
> - Formulate friendly letters to convey ideas related to grade-level experiences/interests
> - Use approved friendly letter format
> - Review and revise their own writing
> - Revise and use editing checklist to edit documents for the traits of quality writing (e.g. ideas, organization, word choice, voice, sentence fluency, conventions and presentation)
> - Prepare and publish final drafts of friendly letters aimed at achieving different purposes — inviting, expressing thanks, requesting

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities

> Students will:
>
> **Pre-writing Activities**
> - List/brainstorm ideas about two given topics, then select one of the topics that they would like to write about. Give reasons for selecting the topic.
> - Select parts of a friendly letter on flash cards prepared by the teacher. Work in groups to place the cards on the structure of a human body (Address, Date, Greetings, Body, Closing). Present their work to the class and explain rationale for the decisions taken within their groups.
> - Peruse samples of letters supplied by the teacher and respond to teacher-guided questions to ascertain the parts and components of a friendly letter. Use information learnt from discussion to note ideas for their own writing.
> - Engage in a teacher-led discussion focused on explaining the RAFT writing strategy (Role, Audience, Format and Topic). Collaborate with teacher to use the RAFT strategy to interpret a given writing task. Document ideas for writing their letter under the Format column of the chart.
>
> **Drafting Activities**
> - Use information from the RAFT chart to compose drafts for their friendly letter.
> - Use a letter template to compose their first draft.
>
> **Revising**
> - Work in groups to peruse and use a teacher-prepared chart with guidelines for revising their drafts. Revise letters using ticks to note parts they are comfortable with and question marks to note areas of concern. Work with teacher/partner to review ticks and question marks. Rewrite draft.
>
> **Editing and Proofreading**
> - Examine the class' existing editing checklists/editing wheel to add or subtract elements to make them appropriate for proofreading friendly letters. Use the revised checklist/editing wheel to edit and proofread their written letters.
> - Work with parents/guardians to source/develop editing checklists for friendly letters. Present their checklist to the class for discussion and feedback.
>
> **Publishing**
> - Place their letters in envelopes and work with their teachers to establish a "postman system" to have their letters delivered.
> - Collaborate with parents/other family members to have their letters hand-delivered/sent by post to their best friend.
> - Collaborate with their teacher to establish a classroom postal agency in the form of a nicely decorated box/chart. Place their letters in the classroom postal agency for further study/reference.

### Key Skills

> List ideas · Brainstorm ideas · Select topic · Identify parts of a friendly letter · Generate ideas · Organize ideas · Document ideas in letter format · Revise written work · Revise checklists · Use checklist to edit work · Publish completed letters

### Assessment

> - Ideas listed are relevant to the topic; reasons given adequately supported choice
> - Charts accurately labelled with the parts of a friendly letter; discussions focused on highlighting and establishing the parts and components of a friendly letter
> - RAFT charts are accurately completed and represent correct interpretation of the writing task; ideas documented under the Format column are aligned to the topic of the letter
> - Draft letter shows good organization of content
> - Letters highlight clear organization of content, appropriate use of transitional words, punctuation and capitalization
> - Checklists are appropriately modified/sourced to suit the revision of the friendly letters; letters are accurately edited
> - Published letters reflect organization of content, appropriate use of transitional words, punctuation and capitalization
