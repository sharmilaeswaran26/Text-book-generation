# Grade 3 — Language Arts — Term 3 — Unit 1

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Language Arts, Term 3, Unit 1 (6 weeks). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Subject:** Language Arts
**Term:** 3
**Unit:** 1
**Duration:** 6 weeks

---

## Introduction to the Unit

As students approach the end of Grade Three, they show signs of maturity and are becoming more responsible for their own learning. In listening and speaking they use the SJE to clarify information and to justify their response to instructions and information. In Word Recognition and Fluency, there is greater focus on structural analysis, with the purpose of using encoding and decoding skills to acquire meaning. As they interface with text, they continue to make connections with self, text and the world. There is in-depth investigation of text features in non-fiction texts.

As students communicate through writing, they employ the skills learnt in process writing. They are exposed to varied strategies that can be used to edit documents. In this unit, the students are given many opportunities to review and revise their writings before publishing.

At the end of this unit, the Grade Three students would have completed three years of the Language Arts Programme at the primary level. They would have exhibited mastery of listening and speaking skills; be exposed to a range of encoding and decoding skills to increase fluency and derive meaning; demonstrated the use of varied reading strategies to gain understanding; and used the structures and mechanics of the SJE learnt, to communicate their ideas through different formats.

Thus, the Grade Three students would have been adequately prepared to begin the Grade Four Language Programme.

*(SJE = Standard Jamaican English; JC = Jamaican Creole.)*

## Strand 1: Speaking and Listening

### Attainment Targets
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively.
- Listen to, recall, understand and respond to speakers' messages, whether implicit or explicit.
- Recognise, value and make distinctions between home language and SJE to improve/acquire language and literacy competences.
- Explain and comment on speakers' use of language, including use of SJE and JC, and their use of vocabulary, grammar and other features.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Ask and answer questions with sensitivity to audience.
- Ask and answer clarifying questions about information presented by peers/teacher.
- Reflect on discussions and talk about how they feel about what was said.
- Make relevant contributions/suggestions to discussion.
- Ask and answer questions to clarify information received.
- Justify response to instruction/information.
- Practise speaking SJE when asking and responding to questions in class.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Select a provider of goods and services and prepare a 3-minute presentation on the roles and responsibilities. Make presentations to the class using SJE. The other students prepare questions to ask at the end of the presentations.
- Select a topic about something that is special to them and share it with the class. Ask and answer questions to gain clarity and explain ideas about the presentation.
- **Observe and Report:** Go on a nature walk. Participate in a discussion that describes the physical environment (trees, houses, roads); compares school and community environments; and shares how to care for and protect the environment.
- In small groups, one student gives an answer to a question that he has made up, without telling the others the question. The other students write and say possible questions. In groups they discuss and decide on the best or most suitable question. Afterwards, they say how they felt when their questions were/were not selected.

### Key Skills
Develop questions · Respond to questions · Speak SJE · Ask and answer questions · Explain ideas · Use SJE · Give accurate descriptions · Compare and contrast · Share factual information · Select questions · Make decisions · Express opinions

### Points to Note
- The teacher guides question-and-answer sessions and reminds students to ask questions that are relevant to the presentation.
- The teacher records incorrect/unsuitable questions for later discussion.

### Assessment
- Questions and answers reflect sensitivity to audience.
- Questions and responses adequately used to clarify information.
- SJE used satisfactorily to ask and answer questions.
- Questions asked and answered are relevant to the presentation.
- Descriptive words used in report accurately capture observations discussed.
- Relevant contributions/suggestions expressed adequately.
- Questions satisfactorily match the answer; descriptive words adequately used to reflect feelings.

## Strand 2: Word Recognition and Fluency

### Attainment Targets
- Read fluently and with appreciation.
- Automatically recognise words (including basic sight word lists) through repeated exposure and mnemonic devices.
- Establish a concept of print and use a range of word recognition clues, re-reading and reading ahead, to identify new words.
- Develop phonic awareness and use knowledge of letter-sound correspondences in order to decode unfamiliar words.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Practise the correct pronunciation of 'x'.
- Read words with vowel digraphs, diphthongs and vowels controlled by 'l', 'r' and 'w'.
- Determine the silent letters in targeted words.
- Read words with silent letters w, l, b, t, k, u and h.
- Apply rules of syllabication in order to identify and read multisyllabic words.
- Apply knowledge of inflectional endings for the purposes of encoding, decoding and deriving meaning.
- Automatically recognise grade level sight words.
- Work cooperatively with peers to build vocabulary.
- Use, with greater effectiveness and accuracy, context clues, pictures, words, sentences and paragraphs as an aid to gain meaning of unfamiliar words.
- Use reference sources to aid in distinguishing between the multiple meanings of targeted words.
- Use knowledge of root words and affixes to determine the meaning of words.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- **Phonics — 'x':** View a video on mouth formation of the consonant 'x', or watch the teacher model the sound /ks/ in isolation and in words. Try to imitate the mouth formations as they pronounce 'x' in isolation and in targeted words, emphasising the ending '-x': *six, wax, box, taxi.*
- **Digraphs, diphthongs and r-controlled vowels:** Take turns drawing cards from a box marked 'Revision' which contains digraphs, diphthongs or r-controlled vowels already learnt, along with associated words. Present the card to the class, produce the sound made in isolation, then read the associated words and assist a peer where challenges are experienced. Examples: ai (hail), ee (feet), igh (high), oa (coat), oo (too, look, poor), ar (Bar), or (For), ur (fur), oi (Oil), ow (down), er (hammer).
- **Silent letters:** Listen to the pronunciation of words with targeted silent letters such as *comb, guard, write, honour,* and mark out the letter on the sound box which is not heard. Share findings with the whole class. In separate lessons, complete offline/online interactive activities with silent letters w, l, b, k, t, h and u as in *thumb, knew, wrap, depot, hour, guide.*
- **Syllabication:** In small groups, review syllabication rules learnt in Grades One and Two and in Terms 1 and 2 of Grade 3. Using erasable markers on whiteboards, indicate syllable junctures with forward slashes in multisyllabic words, e.g. plan/ta/tion, pi/men/to, dif/fer/ent. Read with a pair and apply the strategy during reading.
- **Inflectional endings:** Chunk words into inflectional endings and base words, and give the meaning of the root/base word and the word with the inflectional ending, e.g. *played* (root *play* + *-ed*), *laughing* (root *laugh* + *-ing*), *smiles* (root *smile* + *-s*). Make sentences with the words, e.g. *We played football at the park.*
- **Sight words:** Tour the school environment to find sight words being learnt. Engage in interactive online/offline games. Use a Word Wall to reinforce sight words through games such as 'I Spy'. Play Hopscotch and other physical games. Demonstrate instant recognition of sight words while reading class readers and supplementary texts.
- **Vocabulary development:** View a presentation/chart that reflects the multiple meanings of words used in different contexts. In pairs, read sentences with words having more than one meaning such as *fair, bark, leaves, land, crow* and *bear* (e.g. "Alan was fair in complexion…" / "You are not playing fair…"; "I cannot bear the pain…" / "The bear growled…"). Record and compare each member's thoughts on the meaning of each word.
- **Context clues:** Read sentences, find similar words for highlighted words and underline the words that are context clues, e.g. "The clever dog found a way to open the gate." / "He was a good scholar who got straight A's."
- **Reference sources:** Select word cards from a bank of words with multiple meanings. Using dictionaries, thesauruses and other resources, with teacher and peer support, compose sentences reflecting the multiple meanings.
- **Roots and affixes:** Complete tables in which prefixes/suffixes and roots are used to work out the meanings of words, e.g. *rewrite* (re = again), *dislike* (dis = not), *unhappy* (un = not).

### Key Skills
Imitate mouth formations · Pronounce 'x' in words · Review digraphs/diphthongs/r-controlled vowels · Support peer learning · Identify silent letters · Complete interactive activities · Review syllabication rules · Syllabicate words · Chunk words · Define words · Identify base/root words · Create sentences · Recognise and read sight words · Extract information · Participate in discussion · Determine word meaning · Read sentences · Select similar words · Identify context clues · Peruse reference sources · Compose sentences · Use roots/affixes

### Assessment
- Mouth formations satisfactorily imitated as 'x' is accurately pronounced in isolation and in words.
- Sounds of digraphs, diphthongs and r-controlled vowels accurately produced; associated words accurately read.
- Silent letters accurately identified in targeted words; interactive activities accurately completed.
- Syllabication rules meaningfully reviewed; syllable junctures accurately inserted; words accurately syllabicated during reading.
- Inflectional endings appropriately identified and used to encode, decode and derive meaning.
- Sight words accurately identified in games and automatically recognised during reading; vocabulary satisfactorily revised through the Word Wall and physical games.
- Multiple meanings of targeted words accurately determined; cooperation with peers satisfactory.
- Synonyms correctly selected; context clues correctly identified.
- Reference sources effectively used to compose sentences that distinguish between multiple meanings.
- Affixes and roots used to determine word meanings.

## Strand 3: Comprehension

### Attainment Targets
- Read for meaning, fluency and enjoyment of texts, using a variety of clues to gain information and identify ideas and events.
- Use deduction and inference to interpret information and ideas and to predict outcomes.
- Read fluently and with appreciation.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Reflect and comment on their own progress during the reading process.
- Identify text features in textbooks and nonfiction texts (bold-faced print, italics, diagrams, headings, subheadings, etc.).
- Make text-to-text, text-to-self and text-to-world connections.
- Distinguish between fact and opinion in nonfiction text.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- **Text features:** In small groups, select a flash card with a text feature from a mystery bag. Peruse a grade-level text to identify the chosen feature and discuss its use, then share findings with the class. In pairs, respond to questions focused on using text features to aid comprehension (e.g. "Which text feature would I use to highlight an important word or idea?"). Complete a Feature/Example/Purpose chart (e.g. bold/coloured print — calls attention to new and important words/ideas), or create a strategy poster about a text feature. Complete a Text Feature Hunt and present it to the class.
- **Making connections:** Observe the teacher reading a grade-level text using the Think Aloud strategy and sentence stems such as "This reminds me of…" and "I felt like that character when…" to make connections with self, another text and the real world. Discuss the three levels of connection (text-to-text, text-to-self, text-to-world). Use sentence strips or leaflets to write a sentence or draw a picture depicting a connection and paste it under the appropriate column on a class chart. Plan and present a group dramatization reflecting connections with a given topic. Use a Venn diagram to show connections between two texts read.
- **Fact and opinion:** Engage in a teacher-led discussion that explains the difference between a fact and an opinion. Give examples of statements of fact and opinion about a given topic. In pairs, read a grade-level text and use Post-It notes to identify sentences with a fact or an opinion, and rationalise the decision taken. Use a double-entry journal template to extract and record statements of fact and opinion in one column and reasons in the other. Complete a worksheet writing 'fact' or 'opinion' beside statements extracted from a text. Use the 3-2-1 strategy to identify 3 facts, 2 opinions and 1 idea they are not sure how to classify, and present findings using a 3-2-1 strategy poster.

### Key Skills
Identify text features · Comment on the use of text features · Engage in discussion · Give examples of text features · Prepare strategy poster · Observe demonstration · Make connections (text-to-text, text-to-self, text-to-world) · Use visual representation · Read grade-level texts · Compare texts · Identify facts and opinions · Distinguish between fact and opinion · Justify choices · Listen attentively

### Assessment
- Text features accurately identified in text; explanations focused on the use of the feature.
- Strategy posters depict accurate information regarding the name, purpose and example of the text feature.
- Text Feature Hunt guide accurately completed using the selected text.
- Demonstration and discussion focused on the processes of making connections at all three levels; connections are accurate and reflect the level being represented.
- Completed Venn diagram depicts accurate connections between the two texts read.
- Facts and opinions accurately identified; reasonable rationale given for classification.
- Double-entry journals outline accurate identification of facts and opinions and give valid reasons for the decisions taken.
- Worksheets completed with statements correctly labelled as a fact or an opinion.
- The 3-2-1 strategy poster highlights 3 facts, 2 opinions and 1 idea that students were not certain about based on the text read.

## Strand 4: Grammar and Conventions

### Attainment Targets
- Write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated, using SJE and JC appropriately.
- Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between SJE and JC.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Apply correctly the use of question marks, full stops and commas in a range of written work.
- Apply various forms of verb tense, i.e. present and past tense (regular and irregular) in written and oral work.
- Use the conjunction 'but' to show contrast/differences when constructing simple sentences.
- Apply knowledge of the apostrophe ( ' ) when writing plural possessive nouns.
- Use speech marks/quotation marks in simple dialogue.
- Apply the use of exclamation marks to indicate strong feelings/emotions when composing sentences, e.g. "Stop! You are going to fall."
- Apply the use of the future tense form of verbs using *Will + verb.*
- Use the forms of 'to have', 'to do' and 'to be' to construct sentences (present and past) that can be negated using the negative form 'not', e.g. *has not, have not, had not, do not, does not, did not.*
- Apply the concept of subject-verb agreement with we/they and other plural subjects, e.g. *We write. They listen.*

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- **Punctuation:** In small groups, design a board game which helps them practise using punctuation marks. Develop and record accurately punctuated instructions/rules to play the game. Create a post card message to a real or imaginary friend about a place they like to visit, then analyse and write brief justifications for their choice of punctuation (question marks, full stops, commas).
- **Verb tense:** Rewrite paragraphs from favourite books using another tense (present to past, or past to present) and discuss how the change affects meaning. Attend/watch a sporting event and record events as they unfold using the past tense. Make a running log of irregular verbs in their journals and use them in writing and oral presentations.
- **The conjunction 'but':** Play 'Spin the Wheel' to convert clauses into complete sentences using the conjunction 'but'. Unscramble sentences joined by 'but' and justify their arrangement and the purpose of the conjunction.
- **Plural possessive nouns (apostrophe):** Rewrite phrases so that they include plural possessive nouns, e.g. *the wives of the men, the offices of the teachers.* **Points to Note:** add an apostrophe, e.g. *girls' bags, dogs' tails*; add 's to irregular nouns, e.g. *children's books, men's pants.*
- **Quotation marks:** Collect short comic strips and create a short dialogue based on a chosen strip, using quotation marks to indicate direct speech.
- **Exclamation marks:** Collect non-exclamatory sentences from class Readers and create exclamatory sentences from them, e.g. Reader: "Have you been to Dunn's River Falls?" → Student: "Hurrah! We are going to Dunn's River Falls." Watch a video/photo story of beautiful sites in Jamaica and label each scene with an appropriate exclamatory sentence.
- **Future tense (Will + verb):** Play 'hot seat', asking peers questions about their future plans. Give responses in sentence frames, e.g. *I will + verb…* ("Marcia will eat her dinner.") or *Am/Is/Are + going to + verb…* ("Tommy is going to walk to school.").
- **Negatives:** Convert given affirmative sentences to negative sentences, e.g. *I play football → I do not play football* (using *do not, does not, has not, have not, had not*). Describe events in given pictures using negative sentences. Interview peers about things they do not like, using negative sentences in responses.
- **Subject-verb agreement:** Collect sentences from magazines/newspapers containing plural subjects (we/they) and examine them to establish the rule, e.g. *We write… They listen…* Create a bookmark displaying the subject/verb agreement rule and examples. Complete plural subject/verb agreement worksheets.

### Key Skills
Design board game · Develop instruction/rule sheet · Punctuate written work · Create post card message · Analyse and justify use · Apply correct verb tense · Discuss verb tense · Record events · Use past tense · Create and use lists of irregular verbs · Construct sentences · Use the conjunction 'but' · Rearrange sentences · Justify choices · Use plural possessive forms · Create simple dialogue · Use quotation marks · Collect and construct exclamatory sentences · Ask and respond to questions · Use future tense · Construct negative sentences · Describe pictures · Interview peers · Identify plural subjects · Determine and apply the subject/verb agreement rule · Create bookmarks · Complete worksheets

### Points to Note
- The board game instruction sheet must be accurately punctuated.
- For plural possessive nouns, add an apostrophe (e.g. *girls' bags, dogs' tails*) and add 's to irregular nouns (e.g. *children's books, men's pants*).

### Assessment
- Punctuation board game satisfactorily designed; game instruction sheet correctly punctuated; post card messages correctly punctuated; punctuation use adequately analysed and justified.
- Chosen paragraph rewritten using appropriate verb tense; events recorded accurately with appropriate use of the past tense; list of irregular verbs made and used appropriately.
- Sentences satisfactorily constructed using the conjunction 'but'; scrambled sentences correctly reorganised.
- Phrases rewritten show appropriate use of the apostrophe in plural possessive noun forms.
- Dialogues reflect accurate use of quotation marks.
- Appropriate exclamatory sentences constructed; scenes from the video/photo story satisfactorily labelled.
- Responses to questions reflect accurate use of the future tense.
- Negative sentences accurately formed from given affirmative sentences and in response to peers.
- Sentences analysed and an appropriate conclusion drawn about subject-verb agreement; bookmark creatively designed with accurate rule and examples; worksheets accurately completed.

## Strand 5: Writing

### Attainment Targets
- Develop approaches to the writing process to enable them to organise their ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs.
- Write to narrate, to persuade and for a range of transactional purposes, using SJE and JC appropriately and incorporating multi-media approaches to their writing.
- Use language and text forms appropriately and with imagination to create vibrant and engaging texts.
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them.

### Objectives
Students should be able to:
- Record information learnt through real and vicarious experiences.
- Use a variety of prewriting strategies (e.g. brainstorming, note taking) to organise ideas for writing.
- Modify written pieces to add details.
- Revise and use an editing checklist to edit documents for the traits of quality writing (e.g. ideas, organisation, sentence fluency, conventions and presentation).
- Prepare and publish final drafts of book reports.

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- **Writing to Learn — Observation logs:** Examine a sample observation log and discuss its components and language. In pairs, observe classmates using the various corners (reading corner, nature corner, science corner, etc.) and discuss the type of information that could be recorded and the language used. Complete a sample observation log template. Be guided by the teacher to conduct a nature walk on the school compound or in the community, then use the information gathered to complete an observation log.
- **Writing to Inform — Prewriting:** Examine and discuss sample book reports. Complete a sample report form based on a book previously read, comparing it to the sample for similarities and differences. In pairs, read a grade-level text and identify WOW sentences, paragraphs or pages (information that captures the main and important ideas) using sticky notes.
- **Drafting:** Complete a draft book report template using information gleaned from WOW pages, along with personal reactions.
- **Revising:** Use a teacher-prepared smiley sentence checklist focusing on accuracy of content and inclusion of personal reaction.
- **Editing and Proofreading:** Edit and proofread each other's work as it is projected onscreen or from printed copies. Use the proofreading checklist mounted in class as a guide. Insert a proofreading marker where an area for improvement is noted, and confirm or refute the editing done by classmates through discussion.
- **Publishing:** Source/design a shape of choice using cartridge/construction paper. Mount the completed book report and prepare it for publishing. Submit completed reports to the school's newsletter committee.

### Key Skills
Examine observation log · Engage in discussion · Engage in observation · Complete and conduct observation log · Examine book report · Complete report form · Read texts · Prepare draft book report · Revise work · Use checklist to edit work · Publish completed book report

### Assessment
- Discussion focused on identifying the components and language used in observation logs; observation log completed using information garnered from the activity.
- Completed report form captures essential details in text; WOW sentences, paragraphs and pages identified contain important information and ideas.
- Draft book report shows alignment with information gleaned from WOW pages (main ideas, supporting details); revised report reflects modifications made.
- Proofreading markers accurately inserted to depict areas of concern/for improvement.
- Completed report shows good use of all the elements learnt: main ideas, supporting details, sequencing of ideas, etc.
