# Grade 3 — Mathematics — Content Standard: Geometry

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Mathematics, Content Standard — Geometry. This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Standard 3:** Students will apply geometric concepts (points, paths, lines, shapes) to reason about objects and space in the environment.

---

## Attainment Target

**AT-7** — Explore paths and/or shapes in the environment and relate basic mathematical shapes to everyday life.

---

## Benchmark 3.3.1 — Explore ideas of points, paths, lines, corners, and shapes and relate these to objects in the environment.

### Objectives
- **3.7.1** Identify or describe a point, a line segment, a simple closed path, a polygon, a square corner.
- **3.7.1 (lines)** Identify and name lines, line segments, rays, angles, right angles.

### Illustrative Examples
- Show the square corners in these shapes.

---

## Benchmark 3.3.2 — Identify, describe and name shapes seen in the environment.

### Objectives
- **3.7.4** Describe in words, the differences and similarities between shapes in the environment.
- **3.7.5 (Modified)** Identify similar shapes and objects in the environment and say why they are similar.
- **3.7.3** Name polygons using the names of their corner points in order, e.g., ABCDE, and identify each pair of letters with the appropriate line segment.

### Illustrative Examples
1. Write at least 4 similarities and 2 differences between the square and the rectangle.
2. Discuss the types of triangles, noting the angles and length of the line segments/sides.
3. Examine the triangle, the square and the rectangle, noting the types and number of angles.
4. Draw and name the polygon with 5 angles.
5. Draw and label the vertices of polygons.
6. Name a figure that has a right angle.

---

## Benchmark 3.3.3 — Understand the idea of line symmetry.

### Objectives
- **NEW** Identify by folding, the line of symmetry in objects and shapes.
- **NEW** Check by folding whether or not a given line on an object or shape is a line of symmetry.

### Illustrative Examples
- Cut out the shape. Fold and check to see if the dotted line is a line of symmetry.
