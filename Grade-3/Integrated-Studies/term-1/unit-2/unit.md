# Grade 3 — Integrated Studies — Term 1 — Unit 2

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Integrated Studies, Term 1, Unit 2 (pp. 36–49). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Theme:** Our Needs
**Sub-theme (Grades 1–3 overarching theme):** All About Me and My Environment

**Focus Questions:**
1. What is the difference between basic needs and other needs?
2. How do we satisfy other needs?
3. How does satisfying other needs make a difference to life?

---

## Focus Question 1: What is the difference between basic needs and other needs?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Explain some of the ways in which having a good education helps in satisfying needs

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Mathematics**
- Explore paths and/or shapes in the environment and relate basic mathematical shapes to everyday life.

Objective:
- Identify the line of symmetry in objects and shapes

**Religious Education**
- Recognize that religion contributes to shaping our identity and guides our understanding of the meaning and purpose of life

Objective:
- Explore how religion plays a key role in helping others and providing for their needs

**Drama**
- Express a range of feelings and emotions through the enactment of dramatic scenarios

Objectives:
- Recognize that all persons, regardless of nationality, ethnic background, religious affiliation, etc., have the same desire for the fulfilment of basic needs and other needs
- Use drama modes to differentiate between basic and other needs

**Visual Arts**
- Explore a variety of methods, materials, tools and techniques, including digital art, in the making of art

Objective:
- Use drawing to depict aspects of a song or poem relating to basic needs

**Social Studies**
- Develop an understanding of the interdependent relationship between man and his environment

Objectives:
- Distinguish between basic needs and other needs
- Investigate how persons satisfy basic and other needs

**Physical Education**
- Develop an understanding of how our bodies move, why we move our bodies in particular ways, and what happens to our bodies when they move

Objective:
- Produce creative pieces in response to stimuli in or outside the classroom

**ICT / Language Arts**
- Use technology to communicate ideas and information and work collaboratively
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them

Objectives:
- Communicate information about needs using word-processing software to compose sentences
- Identify and use appropriately adjectives and adverbs when reading a variety of literature and composing simple sentences

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- As a class, discuss the difference between needs and wants. Categorise identified needs into basic needs and other needs. Compose an email or write a letter to their parents/teachers expressing their understanding of basic needs and other needs.
- Take a tour of the school yard and identify things in the environment useful in satisfying basic and other needs. Illustrate these things. Create a flier about how the environment satisfies our needs, with a well-constructed paragraph beside each illustration.
- Use blank sheets of paper, fold them to create origami of things useful in satisfying basic and other needs. Identify the shapes and highlight the lines of symmetry. Place designs in a portfolio.
- Examine and discuss pictures portraying other needs that people have (giving and receiving love and forgiveness, trusting others and being trusted, accepting others and being accepted, giving and receiving comfort, worshipping, keeping safe). Write a paragraph about the needs identified and how they can be addressed. Use word-processing software to compose sentences. Develop a paragraph including sentences about basic and other needs, identifying adjectives and adverbs used.
- Express themselves creatively (song, dance, painting, poem, story, picture collage) in a portrayal of other needs (e.g. friendship, safety, love). Plan and dramatize biblical stories that portray needs, e.g. the story of Esau and Jacob. Discuss the needs portrayed and evaluate the characters' actions.
- Make a journal entry telling what they would like a particular family member to do to fill a need they have. Make a drawing to depict the satisfaction of this need.
- Give examples of references from sacred texts of some of the needs that the Creator fulfils. Classify these needs into two groups — basic needs and other needs.
- Discuss promises made in the Bible and other sacred texts about satisfying our needs (e.g. "my God will meet all your needs according to the riches of his glory in Christ Jesus" — Phil 4:19). Role-play someone who was in doubt about a promise and show how the conflict was resolved, using thought tracking and tableau.
- Create and perform movement sequences to retell any story which depicts the main character's needs and wants. Record the performances and present using presentation software. Critique performances to ascertain if they depicted needs and wants.
- Create a soundscape of environmental sounds and use it to set the mood/tone for a story/drama highlighting how basic and other needs are satisfied. Use found materials to create a backdrop.

### Key Skills
Communicate ideas · Compose emails · Categorise wants and needs · Make observations · Classify objects/things · Write paragraphs · Design fliers · Make origami · Make justifications · Create portfolios · Perform drama pieces · Evaluate · Collaborate · Express creatively · Create journal entries · Make drawings · Role-play · Create movements in response to stimuli · Transfer files between devices · Record presentations · Critique performance · Create soundscapes

### Assessment
- Difference between needs and wants correctly identified; letters and emails accurately composed
- Flier displays accurate information about satisfying needs; paragraphs well-constructed
- Basic and other needs satisfactorily represented in the origami; lines of symmetry accurately identified
- Needs identified and appropriately addressed; sentences composed with correct use of adjectives and adverbs; presentations accurately display other needs
- Drama piece correctly depicts the needs identified from the story
- Drawings satisfactorily depict specified needs
- Examples of needs the Creator satisfies correctly identified; needs correctly classified into basic and other needs
- Role-play appropriately depicts how the conflict was resolved
- Movement sequences and patterns appropriately convey needs and wants
- Story/drama satisfactorily highlights how basic and other needs are met

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of the unit students should be able to:
- Recognise the difference between needs and wants
- Recognise how religions play a key role in helping others to provide for their needs
- Identify pictures displaying basic and other needs
- Use new words and compose sentences relating to basic needs
- Communicate safely with parents and teachers through email
- Create a movement sequence utilizing a combination of patterns
- Identify and list a range of equipment and apparatus used for physical activities
- Observe rules and regulations which govern behaviour in the playing field
- Identify lines of symmetry

### Points to Note
- Ensure that if technology is available it is accessible to each student.
- Point out that the Creator satisfies the needs of the created.
- Ensure that students understand how thought tracking and tableaux are used.

### Extended Learning
- Create a web/tree to depict basic and other needs.

## Focus Question 2: How do we satisfy other needs?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Distinguish between basic needs and other needs

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Language Arts**
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively

Objective:
- Share their own experiences in which friends/other persons/organizations helped them in satisfying needs

**Religious Education**
- Recognize that religion contributes to shaping our identity and guides our understanding of the meaning and purpose of life

Objectives:
- Relate how religions play a key role in satisfying other needs
- Explain the meaning of morals in sacred and other stories
- Recognise the importance of moral values in satisfying other needs

**Drama**
- Explore and create, exercising critical thinking skills throughout the dramatic process; express a range of feelings and emotions through the enactment of dramatic scenarios

Objectives:
- Evaluate the actions of characters in a story in satisfying other needs
- Use various creative expressions to show how needs may be satisfied

**Visual Arts**
- Explore a variety of methods, materials, tools and techniques, including digital art, in the making of art

Objective:
- Use a variety of materials and methods to create art pieces about the satisfaction of other needs

**Social Studies**
- Develop an understanding of the interdependent relationship between man and his environment; know and value the contributions of communities and institutions in fostering national, regional and international integration

Objective:
- Recognise the role of key agencies in satisfying our needs

**ICT**
- Use appropriate digital tools to plan and conduct research; recognise human, social, ethical, cultural and legal issues and practise online safety and ethical behaviour

Objectives:
- Use digital tools to develop a database of organizations with their logos and emergency numbers
- Cite online sources correctly

**Music**
- Create musical compositions exploring varied elements of music; perform varied repertoire of music for an audience

Objective:
- Express themselves creatively in song to indicate the importance of satisfying other needs

**Physical Education**
- Develop an understanding about how our bodies move, why we move our bodies in particular ways, and what happens to our bodies when they move

Objective:
- Create movement sequences from recorded music

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Relate stories from their own experiences in which friends/organizations/other persons assisted them in satisfying needs. Write a letter thanking the friend/organization/other persons for the help rendered.
- Use online/offline sources to collect information on groups or organizations that offer help. Create an information booklet of these agencies and persons; include logos, addresses and telephone numbers which they can access in cases of emergency (e.g. fire, flood, sudden illness, getting lost).
- Invite resource persons from agencies to talk about the role they play in satisfying needs. Write an advertisement for the organization with a jingle. With the teacher's assistance, post the advertisement on the class web page. Write in journals a short report on the speech given by the representative.
- As a whole group, share ideas on how religion plays a key role in satisfying other needs. Listen to/read sacred and other stories that relate to needs (e.g. the Good Samaritan/Cinderella). Identify the need and how it was satisfied. State the importance of holding moral values. Create a collage to illustrate part of the story.
- Work in groups of 4–6 and, using a known tune, produce simple songs from rhymes/jingles to depict how other needs are satisfied. Perform their song for the class.
- Identify and perform cultural songs that tell about satisfying other needs (Colon Man, Under de Coconut Tree, Jane and Louisa, Dis Long Time Gal, Brown Girl in de Ring). Identify the needs expressed and say how they are satisfied.

### Key Skills
Relate stories · Write letters · Research information · Collect data · Create booklets · Create jingles · Write in journals · Write advertisements · Create collages · Perform songs · Compose songs · Listen to/read stories

### Assessment
- Letter correctly constructed
- Completed booklet correctly documents details on organisations
- Creativity of advertisement with jingle appropriately reflects the role of the organization
- Journal entries appropriately made; sentences correctly constructed; collage appropriately illustrates the section of the story selected
- Song/jingle appropriately produced
- Song appropriately depicts satisfying other needs

### Learning Outcomes
Students will be able to:
- Identify ways in which our other needs may be satisfied
- Use a creative expression to show how other needs may be satisfied
- Create a picture collage to illustrate their own experiences in which friends/other persons/organizations helped them in satisfying other needs
- Write a report in their journals
- Produce songs and jingles depicting how other needs are met
- Discuss the needs mentioned in the song they had listened to
- Compile an information booklet of agencies that help to satisfy other needs

### Points to Note
- Ensure that contact is made with resource person/s.
- Ensure that appropriate technology is available for class use.
- Ensure students cite online sources correctly.

### Extended Learning
- Write in their journals poems, verse and songs about ways in which they could love, respect, and forgive their friends.
- Research customs associated with sports in Jamaica.

## Focus Question 3: How does satisfying other needs make a difference to life?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Explain some of the ways in which having a good education helps in satisfying needs

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Language Arts**
- Use and adapt a range of sentence structures according to context, distinguishing between JE and JC; write sentences which are grammatically accurate and correctly punctuated; use a range of punctuation correctly

Objective:
- Develop simple sentences stating how other needs are satisfied

**Religious Education**
- Recognize that religion contributes to an understanding of human needs and other needs

Objective:
- Explain specific ways in which people satisfy their need for love, trust, worship, friendship, etc.

**Drama**
- Express a range of feelings and emotions through the enactment of dramatic scenarios

Objective:
- Demonstrate ways by which individuals are dependent on each other, especially during life-threatening situations

**Social Studies**
- Develop an understanding of the interdependent relationship between man and his environment

Objectives:
- Explore the expressed attributes of people and objects in order to satisfy other needs
- Explain the positive and negative ways in satisfying other needs

**Music**
- Create musical compositions exploring various elements of music

Objective:
- Use call and response techniques to create simple musical compositions about satisfying other needs

**ICT**
- Use technology to design and produce multimedia products to demonstrate their creative thinking

Objective:
- Use appropriate presentation software to create and record presentations

**Physical Education**
- Explore simple locomotor, non-locomotor actions, pathways and levels with basic control and coordination

Objective:
- Use movement skills — locomotor, non-locomotor, manipulation of equipment, space, and qualities of movement — to communicate ideas and feelings

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Identify organisations on which individuals can depend in order to cope with life-threatening situations. List the names of these organisations under the headings Governmental/Non-Governmental. Discuss the role each organisation plays in satisfying a particular need that has made a difference to life. Relate experiences about life-threatening situations and how they or others were helped. Use ICT tools to voice-record their experiences and save for playback.
- Use tableau/thought tracking to depict situations in which the needs of love, trust, respect and friendship were met. Record the drama presentation and play back for class discussion. Create a lyrical composition informing the class of how satisfying other needs makes a difference to life, using call-and-response, clapping of hands and stomping of feet.
- Read sacred stories about how satisfying other needs made a difference in people's lives (e.g. the Biblical account of the man at the Gate Beautiful). Perform a movement sequence to demonstrate how the reaction of the character(s) made a difference to their lives. Create various art pieces that express and convey these messages.
- Work in groups to discuss how individuals depend on each other during crises/life-threatening situations such as fire, accident, crossing a flooded river, lightning, or water lock-off in a nearby community. Use drama modes to depict their group presentations.
- Conduct a survey to identify a need within their community that needs addressing. Plan a community-based project to address the need. Strategize a way of solving the problem to make a significant difference to the lives of the people in that community. Create a display highlighting the plan in its entirety.

### Key Skills
Categorise organisations · Discuss roles · Relate experiences · Record experiences · Dramatize situations · Discuss ideas · Create rhythmic patterns · Read stories · Create art pieces · Perform movement sequences · Collaborate · Conduct surveys · Strategize to solve problems · Create displays

### Assessment
- Organizations correctly categorised; content/information correctly relayed with clarity
- Needs met accurately presented; rhythmic patterns appropriately created; performance of composition appropriately executed
- Art pieces satisfactorily convey messages about how satisfying other needs makes a difference to life
- Scenarios correctly reflect interdependence
- Display satisfactorily highlights the need and the plan to resolve it

### Learning Outcomes
Students will be able to:
- Identify organisations on which individuals can depend to make a difference to life
- Dramatize various scenes effectively, conveying the intended message
- Recognise the need to work together to address needs
- Identify and explain the positive and negative ways in satisfying others' needs
- Recognise how art can be used to express emotions and feelings
- Recognise that some life-threatening situations can be alleviated by planning and cooperation
