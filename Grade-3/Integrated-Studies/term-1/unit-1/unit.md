# Grade 3 — Integrated Studies — Term 1 — Unit 1

> **Faithful curriculum extract** from the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum (NSC), Grade 3, Integrated Studies, Term 1, Unit 1 (pp. 18–35). This file preserves the curriculum as written for teachers. The student-facing lesson is in `textbook.md`.

**Theme:** My Body
**Sub-theme (Grades 1–3 overarching theme):** All About Me and My Environment

**Focus Questions:**
1. Why are teeth, stomach and lungs important parts of my body?
2. Why and how should I care for the different parts of my body?
3. How does food help to make my body healthy?

---

## Focus Question 1: Why are teeth, stomach and lungs important parts of my body?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Identify some internal organs of the body
- Recall their learning experiences in *My Body Part 2* in Grade 2

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Science**
- Explore the attributes/characteristics/features of people and objects in the environment in order to classify and make comparisons.
- Begin to understand and apply aspects of the scientific method.
- Explore the properties of various materials, selected forces and forms of energy through the use of the senses.

Objectives:
- Identify the different types of teeth
- Identify the external parts of a tooth
- Distinguish between temporary (primary, milk, baby) and permanent teeth
- Locate the stomach and lungs in the human body
- Describe the appearance/structure of the stomach and lungs — size, shape and colour
- Describe changes in the chest as air enters and leaves the lungs
- Investigate the functions of the teeth, stomach and lungs
- Demonstrate, using simple apparatus, how the lungs work
- Investigate the elastic nature of the stomach

**Language Arts**
- Write well-constructed paragraphs which have linking sentences within and between them
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively

Objective:
- Relate information about the teeth, stomach and lungs using well-constructed paragraphs

**ICT / Visual Arts**
- Students use digital tools to design and produce creative multimedia products to demonstrate their learning and understanding of basic technology operations.
- Plan and design Visual Arts expressions

Objectives:
- Use ICT tools to create a multimedia presentation on teeth
- Create models of the lungs, stomach and a tooth using a variety of materials

**Religious Education**
- Recognise that religion contributes to shaping our identity and guides our understanding of the meaning and purpose of life

Objective:
- Relate the religious story of 'Jonah and the Whale' to the elastic nature of the stomach

**Drama**
- Explore and create, exercising critical thinking skills, throughout the dramatic process

Objective:
- Use drama modes to portray the functions of the teeth, stomach and lungs

**Music**
- Create musical compositions exploring varied elements of music
- Perform varied repertoire of music

Objectives:
- Create jingle about the teeth, stomach and lungs
- Use body percussion and instruments to apply the correct rhythm/beat/tempo/timing
- Use appropriate breathing exercises to produce a variety of long, detached and smooth sounds

**Mathematics**
- Read and interpret information presented in tables or graphs
- Use the basic operations with numbers and number patterns

Objective:
- Construct simple tables or graphs to record data about the teeth, stomach and lungs

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Using a mirror, make observations of their teeth and talk about the location of the different types of teeth. Record which of their temporary (milk, baby, primary) teeth are still present.
- View a chart/video/multimedia presentation on the external parts of a tooth or examine preserved specimens. Identify the crown and root of each type of tooth, record any differences and share findings with the class.
- Watch video/examine specimens or pictures of intact teeth — temporary (primary, milk, baby) and permanent in the upper and lower jaw. In groups make observations about their shape, texture, size, number and position and record information. Count the number of each type of teeth in each picture and record information in a table, then respond to teacher-generated questions about the teeth. Use an appropriate ICT drawing tool or draw and colour/paint, and label the crown, root and enamel of the tooth.
- Eat samples of own food brought from home to identify the function of each type of tooth. Discuss, in groups, the chewing process and the functions of each type of tooth (include common names — e.g. front teeth = incisors (biting/cutting), 'eye' teeth = canines (tearing and gripping), 'jaw' teeth = molars (crushing/grinding)).
- In groups, use presentation software to design a creative multimedia product/handout showing the types of teeth and their functions. Complete teacher-generated worksheet and crossword puzzle about the teeth.
- Sing/create songs about the types and functions of teeth. Use body percussion and classroom instruments to accompany singing. Count and clap steady beats to the song.
- From a variety of materials create models of tooth/stomach/lungs (modelling clay, play dough, etc.). Write a paragraph using well-constructed sentences and appropriate punctuation to describe the models.
- Discuss the importance of taking care of their teeth. Listen to the instructions of the dentist; if possible take a field trip to the dentist.
- Use their bodies to show the approximate position of the stomach. Watch video/examine picture/chart of the stomach. In groups, discuss the functions, location and appearance of the stomach. Drink 2–3 cups of water and tell its effect on the stomach.
- In small groups, carry out investigations using balloons to show some functions of the stomach (e.g. to accommodate food). Record the process and findings in a paragraph.
- Read the story of Jonah and the Fish. View pictures with Jonah in the stomach of the fish. Discuss how Jonah was able to fit in the whale's stomach and present the information creatively. Create jingles about the role of the stomach.
- Use their bodies to indicate the approximate position of lungs. Watch video/examine picture/chart of the lungs. In groups, discuss the functions, location and appearance. Inhale and exhale large puffs of air. Observe, comment on and record changes in the chest.
- Conduct a simple investigation using balloons, a plastic bottle, straws and elastic bands to demonstrate how the lungs work. Write a paragraph/poem/jingle about the lungs.
- Conduct breathing exercises to develop techniques for singing and playing — broken (staccato) then smooth sounds (legato). Create simple melodies incorporating ostinatos (repeated patterns).

### Key Skills
Make observations · Report to class · Record information · Label parts of the tooth · Draw and colour · Design multimedia product · Create songs · Maintain steady beat · Create models · Develop paragraphs · Investigate using balloons · Listen to story · Conduct investigations · Practice controlled breathing · Create melodies

### Assessment
- Different types of teeth accurately recorded; crown and root correctly identified
- Differences between temporary and permanent teeth correctly identified; crown, root and enamel correctly labelled
- Types of teeth correctly linked with their role(s) in the chewing process
- Information in handout relevant and accurate; presentation shows originality and creativity; worksheets and crossword puzzle accurately completed
- Models adequately represent tooth, stomach and lungs; sentences well-constructed; punctuation correctly used
- Approximate position of the stomach satisfactorily identified; function, location and appearance correctly discussed
- Effects of additional water in the stomach adequately described; investigation accurately recorded
- Jingles appropriately reflect the role of the stomach
- Functions, location and appearance of lungs accurately represented; changes in the chest during breathing appropriately recorded
- Demonstration of how the lungs work correctly done
- Breathing techniques correctly executed; melodies accurately produced

### Learning Outcomes
Students will be able to:
- Indicate type and location of teeth and parts of a tooth
- Know the difference between temporary and permanent teeth
- Explain the functions of each type of tooth
- Explain the functions of the stomach/lungs
- Draw conclusions from experiments about the functions of the teeth
- Explain the changes in the chest cavity when inhaling and exhaling
- Manipulate clay, play dough, etc. to create models of teeth and lungs
- Work collaboratively to produce a multimedia product on how to care for our teeth
- Use body percussion and instruments to apply the correct rhythm/beat/tempo/timing to jingles created

### Points to Note
- A class book about the internal organs of the body will be kept for upcoming weeks.
- Conduct experiments before class to ensure they are workable.
- Ensure there is a variety of materials for students to work with when making models.
- Ensure all students have a chance to use digital tools.
- Ostinatos are short rhythmic/melodic patterns repeated through a piece of music.
- Teacher can generate crosswords and puzzles.
- Wherever activities indicate teeth/stomach/lung, treat each in a separate lesson.
- The multimedia product could include a video demonstrating functions of teeth.

### Extended Learning
- Create a TV or radio ad with a jingle to show how to care for the teeth.
- Form an ensemble and perform at a school function.

## Focus Question 2: Why and how should I care for the different parts of my body?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Demonstrate knowledge of the importance of the teeth, stomach and lungs

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Science**
- Begin to appreciate the variety of living things and how they are supported by the environment.
- Begin to explore the environment in order to relate everyday experiences to simple scientific concepts and processes using/developing models that represent concrete events.

Objectives:
- Explain why we need to care for different parts of our bodies
- Illustrate ways in which we should care for parts of our body
- Demonstrate ways we care for our teeth
- Differentiate between healthy and unhealthy habits
- Recognize the need to care for oneself through living a healthy lifestyle

**Language Arts**
- Communicate with confidence and competence for different purposes and audiences, using SJE and JC appropriately and creatively
- Develop approaches to the writing process to organise ideas into a coherent structure including layout, sections and paragraphs

Objective:
- Use simple sentences to communicate ideas about caring for parts of the body

**Religious Education**
- Display an appreciation of some of the beliefs, practices and expressions of persons from different religious faiths

Objective:
- Research religious sayings about the care of the body

**Physical Education**
- Develop the understanding and skills that support healthy, safe, and active individuals

Objectives:
- Explain how exercise keeps their bodies healthy
- Identify short-term effects of exercise/physical activity on the body

**Drama / ICT**
- Explore and create, exercising critical thinking skills throughout the dramatic process; express a range of feelings and emotions through the enactment of dramatic scenarios
- Use digital tools to design and produce creative multimedia products

Objectives:
- Use a drama mode to demonstrate the effects of not taking care of the teeth
- Use ICT tools to communicate information by entering text and graphics to create a flier/handout

**Music / Visual Arts**
- Create musical compositions exploring varied elements of music; perform varied repertoire of music for an audience
- Demonstrate the creation of art through the use of tools, themes and materials

Objectives:
- Sing or compose songs about caring for the body
- Use body percussion and instruments to create accompaniment for jingles
- Maintain steady beat patterns
- Explore a variety of methods, materials, tools and techniques, including digital art, in the making of art

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Sing the song 'My Head, My Shoulders, My Knees, My Toes'. Discuss and answer questions about why and how to care for the parts of the body. Write a 'thank you' note to the Creator and present in the form of dub poetry. Talk about healthy and unhealthy habits. List six unhealthy things done the previous day/week, discuss and identify ways to correct them.
- Discuss the care of the body (hygiene, diet, exercise, rest). Create tableaux depicting persons caring for the body. Construct and print a class letter to the nearest Health Centre requesting a resource person to speak about caring for themselves. Record the presentation for class discussion. In groups, use a digital tool to design a poster/flier/handout that discourages eating junk food and encourages eating healthy foods.
- Use previously created models to show how to care for the teeth. Sing the song 'Brush your teeth' to a popular tune with body percussion and classroom instruments.
- In role as dentist/dental hygienist/dental technician, state what he/she does at the clinic. Capture the presentation and play back for class discussion.
- Do exercise/physical activity (e.g. run on the spot for 30 seconds, then bend and touch toes ten times). Talk about how the body responds (sweating, increased breathing, increased pulse, tiredness) and how the body benefits. Examine multimedia materials of athletes; discuss and record in tabular form advantages/disadvantages of exercising.
- Pretend to be someone with a problem relating to the teeth, stomach or lungs. Use an art form (short poem, song, mime, dance) to convey: what the problem is; how it could have been prevented (healthy eating/diet); how it can be remedied.
- Work in small groups (with the teacher's assistance) to research and prepare a set of simple sayings from different religions about keeping our bodies healthy. Make a decorated sticker for each saying. Compose songs/poems about the sayings, practising long and short sounds when singing.
- Design (individually or in groups) a board game (e.g. 'snakes and ladders' with 'forward' moves for positive responses and 'backward' moves for negative responses) based on behaviours that show caring for the body. Make a set of 'caring for my body' cards depicting negative and positive behaviour.
- Compile a portfolio using pictures of items that are used to care for the body. Use discarded materials to make these items.

### Key Skills
Discuss information · Write notes/letters · List habits · Share information · Record presentation · Design poster/flier · Communicate information · Keep steady beat · Compose and perform jingles/songs · Create presentation · Dramatize roles · Create tables · Discuss benefits of exercise · Create artistic pieces · Research sayings · Design game · Make cards · Compile portfolio · Create prototypes

### Assessment
- Questions responded to accurately; activities correctly identified as healthy or unhealthy; letter satisfactorily constructed
- Models satisfactorily constructed; posters creatively designed with accurate information; information correctly entered in poster/flier/handout
- Jingles/songs satisfactorily created; rhythmic patterns synchronised
- Digital presentation properly developed; role of dental personnel correctly depicted
- Advantages and disadvantages of exercising accurately recorded
- Problem, its prevention and remedy adequately portrayed in artistic piece
- Religious sayings adequately represented in poems/songs
- Games accurately portray healthy and unhealthy behaviour/practices
- Portfolio appropriately compiled; items created show correct representation

### Learning Outcomes
Students will be able to:
- Recognize habits that have healthy and unhealthy effects
- Appreciate how and why we need to care for different parts of our bodies
- Recognize the effects of exercise on the body
- Identify long and short sounds in songs and use their voices effectively
- Use long and short sounds to create a variety of movement patterns
- Combine graphics and texts to enhance design
- State advantages and disadvantages of exercising
- Create jingles/songs about specific parts of the body
- Make decorated stickers of religious sayings

### Points to Note
- Ensure that resources (video/digital presentations) are prepared in advance of the lesson.

### Extended Learning
- Interview a family/community member to ascertain the methods used in the past to care for their bodies; then make a tabular comparison to how it is done now.

## Focus Question 3: How does food help to make my body healthy?

### Prior Learning
Check that students can:
- Explain how to care for different parts of their bodies

### Attainment Targets and Objectives

**Science**
- Explore some/selected attributes of people and objects in the environment in order to classify and make comparisons.

Objectives:
- Identify foods according to their groups and nutrients
- Explain ways in which foods help to keep us healthy
- Identify some problems which result from not eating a balanced diet
- Identify the key ways in which food can keep our bodies healthy
- Identify habits that have healthy and unhealthy effects/consequences

**Language Arts**
- Apply relevant decoding skills to decode words from key vocabulary

Objective:
- Use adjectives correctly when creating well-constructed sentences and paragraphs

**Mathematics**
- Use the correct units, tools and attributes to estimate, compare and carry out the processes of measurement to a given degree of accuracy.

Objective:
- Identify units of measure for items bought or sold

**Physical Education**
- Develop the understanding and skills that support healthy, safe, and active individuals

Objective:
- Recognise the need for food and fluid to support physical activity

**Visual Arts / Drama**
- Demonstrate the creation of art through the use of tools, themes and materials
- Explore and create, exercising critical thinking skills throughout the dramatic process

Objectives:
- Explore a variety of methods, materials, tools and techniques, including digital art, in the making of art
- Use various drama modes to demonstrate how food makes our bodies healthy

**ICT**
- Use technology to communicate ideas and information and work collaboratively; use appropriate digital tools to plan and conduct research; recognise human, social, ethical, cultural and legal issues and practise online safety.

Objectives:
- Use a digital device to communicate information on food nutrients, balanced diet, obesity and rickets
- Use digital tools to gather information on food nutrients, balanced diet, obesity and rickets

**Music**
- Create musical compositions exploring varied elements of music

Objective:
- Create jingles representing the importance of healthy foods

**Resource & Technology**
- Use simple materials, tools and equipment to develop skills in cutting, assembling and decorating

Objective:
- Explore ways of preparing food and explain hygienic practices to be observed in the preparation

**Religious Education**
- Display an appreciation of some of the beliefs, practices and expressions of persons from different religious faiths

Objectives:
- Investigate reasons given for food-related practices among religious groups
- Describe some dietary customs which conform to guidelines for good health rooted in religious practices

### Suggested Teaching and Learning Activities
Students will:
- Collect and examine pictures of foods and place them into the food groups: staples, legumes, fruits and vegetables, fats and oils, and food from animals. Construct a table to record the food groups and examples, and write short sentences stating how the foods in each group contribute to good health (food for 'go' = energy; 'grow' = building and maintaining the body; 'glow' = regulating body processes). Use the cut-out pictures to make a collage about healthy foods. Sing songs about the food groups.
- Create a digital story/role-play to show how food helps to keep the body healthy. Record the drama piece and play back for class discussion.
- In small groups, research food nutrients and a balanced diet using online/offline sources. Make a journal entry showing a plate with drawings depicting a balanced diet. Use vox pop to share the information orally.
- Plan a field trip to the market to observe various foods on a pre-prepared list. State the units of measure suitable for selected items. Purchase a few food items (especially fruits and vegetables). Observe food colour and texture. Identify which foods are usually eaten cooked and which uncooked. Capture images of the field trip and foods.
- Be guided by the teacher in preparing a simple dish (e.g. fruit salad) or meal (e.g. cheese sandwich). Demonstrate proper hand washing and ensure ingredients, utensils and working areas are clean.
- Examine pictures/videos of malnourished children/adults; observe shape and size of body parts, condition and colour of skin and hair. Discuss the effects of lack/excessive intake of food from particular food groups. Write a descriptive paragraph or create an artwork depicting someone who has had either too much or too little food.
- Run on the spot for 30 seconds, then do jumping jacks. Talk about how they feel (sweaty, thirsty) and how the effects can be remedied.
- View videos in which persons from Christian and Jewish religious groups describe some common dietary religious practices, giving simple explanations. Record information in the class journal. In groups, prepare a menu card for each religious group.
- Conduct a survey to find out the types of food consumed at school each day by students in their class. Construct a bar graph. Discuss the nutritive value of each food. Create a jingle encouraging students to eat healthy foods.

### Key Skills
Observe pictures · Categorize foods · Construct tables · Communicate information · Create collage · Sing with accuracy · Create digital story · Record drama piece · Research food nutrients and balanced diet · Write journal entries · Make observations · Identify and sort/classify foods · Collaborate and cooperate · Cut out pictures · Write descriptive paragraphs · Create art pieces · Record information · Create menu cards · Collect data · Construct graphs · Analyse data · Draw conclusions · Create jingles

### Assessment
- Food samples correctly categorized into food groups; statements accurately written on the role of foods
- Pictures in collage appropriately depict healthy foods
- Digital story/role-play correctly reflects how food helps to keep the body healthy
- Information on food nutrients and balanced diet accurately presented; illustration on plate correctly reflects a balanced diet
- Units of measure correctly identified; foods eaten cooked and uncooked correctly identified
- Dishes include two or more food groups; proper hygiene demonstrated in the preparation of meals; cleanliness properly practised throughout
- Final product creatively presented; paragraphs well-constructed; effects of lack/excessive intake of food correctly identified; art piece well designed
- Common religious dietary practices accurately identified and explained; menu card contains foods appropriate for religious groups
- Bar graph accurately presents data on foods consumed by the class; jingles appropriately promote healthy eating habits

### Learning Outcomes
Students will be able to:
- Identify foods according to their groups and nutrients
- Explain ways in which foods help to keep us healthy
- Identify problems that result from not eating a balanced diet
- Identify units of measure for items bought or sold
- Demonstrate hygienic practices in food preparation
- Describe dietary customs rooted in religious practices
- Collect data, construct a bar graph and draw conclusions about the nutritive value of foods
